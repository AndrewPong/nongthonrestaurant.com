The Rich & the Fabulous: The Remake

by Bunny Ears

Chapter Five - Lunch with the Rich & Fab

 

*******************

 

Daniel was talking nonstop by her side as they marched toward the campus' canteen. Gillian was surprised what a humble and outgoing guy he actually was and couldn't help but feel a bit lucky to know that he was really her brother. However, upon the recall of Edison's words, she felt a little guilty. It was not her intention to make him fall for her--god, what a horrible idea that was--but the truth was right into her face. Daniel clearly harbored some type of fondness toward her.

 

She had to distance herself from him before he could fall deeper into the hole of doom.

 

"Hey, Gil, why won't you sit with us?" Daniel offered, pointing toward a big crowd of table where a group of richly dressed folks lounged. Ruby was amongst one of them.

 

"Don't kid me, Daniel," Ruby said with a sneer as she crossed her arms. "She's not a rich, nor is she a fabulous. Like, hello? Out of range, here?"

 

As much as the scorn in her tone grated her, Gillian knew that what she said was really true. Plus, the idea of meddling into a group she did not belong to didn't sound all too appealing anyway. "Yeah, she's right, Daniel. I'm just going to sit over there by myself."

 

"I'll come with you." Charlene's declaration of withdrawal from the group surprised pretty much everyone, but Gillian was amazed at the girl's bravery.

 

"See what you've done?" One of the guys at Ruby's table spoke up with an irritating tone. "Why don't you try to be nice for a change?"

 

"Oh, shut your trap, Kenny," Ruby sneered disdainfully, flipping her hair in the air in a snobby gesture. "Haven't we discussed the rules already? A poor girl like her doesn't deserve to sit with us."

 

"Actually, I don't deserve to suffer with a bunch of snobs," Gillian defended harshly as Ruby's eyes enlarged in comprehensible outrageousness. This girl knew no boundaries and the more Gillian saw or heard of her, the more she grew sick. She could care less about mingling into that kind of crowd. Perhaps it was a blessing not to. "Anyway, let's go, Charlene."

 

Kenny whistled a tune, admiring the girls' will of resistance, but was disrupted when Ruby stomached him painfully with her elbow. "Shit. That hurts. How many times do I have to tell you I'm not your damn punch bag?"

 

"Oops, should I say sorry then?" Ruby asked sarcastically.

 

"If that is the price you're paying me, no thanks," Kenny gnarled.

 

Edison studied from the side with watchful eyes as Gillian and Charlene marched their way out of the scene. He had to admit, she was pretty damn confident for her own good but another part of him quickly scolded himself. You do not admire a witch! He hissed inside, suddenly realizing what nonsense he was whipping up. His eyes continued to linger on them as he ambled toward his seat but because he eyed them with such intensity, he missed his chair and fell flat on his butt.

 

"Ow!"

Everyone's eyes glided over to Edison, who sprawled on the floor. No one spoke a word.

That was, until Kenny burst out laughing.

 

Ruby sprung from her seat and came to his aid. "You okay, Edison? You should be careful!!"

 

"Uh, yeah, I'm okay," Edison was trying to say but Ruby kept blabbering about how he should watch out for this, and watch out for that. He felt many eyes were on him and for someone as confident as he was, he had to feel the rising heat of embarrassment crawling and even staining his cheeks. "Come on! It was just a fall! Big fucking deal."

 

Kenny was smiling. "You should have seen the look on your face. Big deal, all right."

 

"Shut up!" Edison hissed.

 

"Come on, Edison. Get up on your feet and stop putting up a scene," Daniel berated from the side.

 

"Uh, yeah, I'm not trying to?" Edison rolled his eyes. God, it was that witch’s entire fault. If it weren't for her, he wouldn't have his head lost in the clouds somewhere. Urg, she was nothing but troubles, an unspeakable omen. "Can you please move?" He hissed at Ruby who then pouted at his strong reaction when she was merely trying to help.

 

Ruby had on a guilty face as Edison helped himself up and sat his still hurting butt on the seat. When his eyes darted to the table across from them, he could see the witch laughing at something. Hell, it wasn't at him was he? Why that--

 

"Edison, Edison, how can you be so clumsy. Look, you brought attention on us," another guy Takeshi  Kaneshiro commented. But his tone sounded anything but bothered by the attention. He even waved hello to the surrounding patrons, actually enjoying their eyes on him.

 

"Get a grip," Ruby retorted vexingly before turning slightly to her right to face Edison. "So what do you want to eat?"

 

While Edison was glancing down at the menu, Daniel diverted his gaze to Gillian. True, he was disappointed that she wasn't joining them, and true he wished he could go over there, but wouldn't that make him so obvious in his intentions? He didn't want to scare her away.

 

"Why does he get to make the decisions?" Takeshi asked, annoyed. "Yo, waiter, you!" He beckoned a nearby man. "Get me this Hot Grizzo, Hot Guy on Boat, some of these appetizers, and a cool glass of Iceberg to help tame down the heat," Takeshi fanned himself. "Wow, I'm so hot."

 

"Tackie, do us a favor!" Charlene yelled from not so far away, catching on nearby attention. Customers were getting irritated at the young adults’ nonstop rowdiness. "Kill yourself, please?"

 

Takeshi slapped on an anxious look. "Oh no, I can't do that. I'm too handsome to die."

 

Daniel groaned with complete annoyance. He wasn't into this nonsense at all. All he could think about was Gillian. This did it. He couldn't stomach this boredom anymore that he decided to abandon his pride for a moment and just saunter towards the two ladies. "Mind if I sit here?" he asked, full of nervousness.

 

Charlene gazed at him, smiling mischievously. It was dumb of anyone to think that she couldn't smell a thing. "Of course I do. No boys allowed at this table."

 

"Come on, you're not anti-men so stop acting like one," Daniel rebutted and situated himself onto the seat across from Gillian, apparently determined to stay there. "So, what have you two ordered?"

 

"Salads," Gillian answered. "Don't look at me like that. It was her decision." Charlene beamed with a smile as if she were a proud parent.

 

"You're kidding me. At this rate, you two are going to turn into celery sticks!" Daniel said in outrage, not believing the girls' absurdity. With a loud voice, he beckoned for the waiter to come. "Get me these steak dishes and hamburgers. The more buttery and thicker they are, the better."

 

"The more buttery and thicker they are, the better," Charlene mimicked with a high pitch, obviously not liking his decisions. "I guess when I become a fat lady, I should thank you, father."

 

Daniel smiled, dismissing her sarcasm. "You're welcome."

 

Gillian could feel Edison's gaze burned deeply on her. It seemed almost as if he was squinting his eyes and jabbing a fork at her as though to say 'I'm watching you'. She couldn't hold in the urge to roll her eyes. That guy thought she was some sort of evil demon from hell. Well, if that was what he thought, she might as well fulfill her role. "Daniel," she crooned, "have anyone ever told you you're so hot?" She made sure Edison heard it from a table away.

 

Daniel's cheeks flushed red immediately. He didn't want to admit it because that'd make him sound arrogant, so he just humbly said, "Well, you're the first one."

 

Gillian laughed loudly and she could see Edison's expression twitched. "That's a surprise. Normally, guys like you should have dozens of girls fawn around you."

 

Daniel scratched the back of his head. "Well, I don't know... I'm not the player type..." He clarified, making sure she got it.

 

Edison's nose flared as Gillian flashed him triumphant eyes. That witch! How dared she try to pawn on her own brother! How utterly disgusting! Edison couldn't take in the sight anymore and had to stomp over to their table without any manners. Well, it wasn't like he had any in the first place anyway. "Hey, I'm joining you guys."

 

Daniel gazed at Edison with annoyance, obviously thinking that he must be jealous of his conversation with Gillian. But he knew he was supposed to be fair since they did agree to a fair competition. "Suit yourself."

 

Edison didn't need Daniel's approval before he sat himself onto a seat. "Okay, so what were you guys talking about that was so funny?" Edison asked, eyes boring into Gillian. His tone dripped with an apparent sharpness.

 

Befuddlement scathed Charlene as she tried to figure out the situation. She had gotten hints that these two had something with Gillian earlier. Edison must have been jealous to discard his big ego to scuttle over here for a seat, but yet, from the acuteness of his tone, it sounded as if he were enemies with her instead. Charlene couldn't comprehend what was going on.

 

Ruby from the other table flared with discontent as she saw her so-called boyfriend engaged in a conversation. How dared he desert her like this! Not withstanding the nerve-pricking sight, she decided to sashay over to their table, leaving Kenny and Takeshi to talk among themselves.

 

Ruby's presence made Daniel groan in annoyance. It seemed that he was pulling the whole table all over here! "Eddie," she said.

 

Edison almost shuddered at her sudden touch. It bothered him the way she still behaved as though she were his girlfriend. These past weeks, he only put up with her because he was being nice, knowing that she needed time to adjust. However, he had had enough. Surely, those people's eyes on him weren't helping either. "Rubes, get your hands off. I'm not your boyfriend." Edison said, trying to maintain his cool.

 

Ruby's face flushed, gashed by Edison's sudden coldness. "What are you talking about?"

 

"Come on, Rubes," Edison rolled his eyes. He wanted to make it gentle, but her reluctance to believe reality grated him. To say he was the most patient person on earth wasn't something quite reliable. "We went out, we had it good. Now we're over. So move on."  He was always one who was straightforward and couldn't be bothered with something called courtesy.

 

"Eddie!" Ruby shrieked. "How can you say that? We went out for months and you're just going to end it here?! Why!?"

 

"No, I think you misunderstood. I broke up with you like weeks ago?" Edison reminded her.

 

"Oh my god. You were serious? I thought you were playing!" Ruby said, shocked and horrified. For a moment there, Gillian felt sorrow for her at the sight of accumulation of tears in her eyes.

 

"Uh no, I don't think I was."

 

"No. You were!" Ruby screamed, her pride apparently too huge to swallow up the fact that she wasn't good enough. "You just changed your mind all of the sudden. Is it... Is it her?!" Ruby pointed at Gillian, who in return sent her a baffled look. "I knew it! The moment I saw you to walking in together, I knew you two had something!"

 

Charlene's mouth opened with fascination. She seemed to be enjoying the show.

 

"Come on, don't be such a drama--" Edison couldn't finish when--

 

SLAP!

 

His face snapped to the side instantly, a burning sting enveloping his cheek. He turned around to face a fuming Ruby.

 

"You... You cheated on me!" She proclaimed hysterically.

 

Although Gillian didn't think the slap was appropriate, she believed that Edison deserved it, and subconsciously, she couldn't help but smile sadistically at his well earned punishment. Edison caught her sly grin, however.

 

"Hey!" Edison hissed, dignity insulted and pride wounded, especially when everyone tossed him deragotory looks. "You have no right to slap me!"

 

"You're right!" Ruby shrieked but her tone was anything but apologetic. "I should slap her instead!" She approached Gillian and was about to slap the astonished girl to hell when Daniel abruptly shot up from his seat and grabbed her wrist just in the nick of time.

 

"Calm down, Rubes!" Daniel held the angry girl back. "We're in a restaurant for God's sake!"

 

Ruby was far too blinded by repulsion to give a damn. Her rationality had already thinned off when Edison declared their expired relationship. "I don't care! I will slap this bitch!"

 

Gillian shot up from her seat; feelings bore for this woman before her deteriorated at once. "If you think your assumptions were right, then there must be something seriously wrong with your brain."

 

Ruby shot her a glare full of hate, affront slapping her cheeks raw. "How... how dare you!" She screamed indignantly. "I will teach you what manners are and how to act like a woman! How dare you mess with my boyfriend, you whore--"

 

Ruby's unfound insult ignited Gillian's violent side as she grabbed the glass of juice and splashed the cold content onto Ruby without a considerable thought, making her shriek with total astound and horror. Everyone around watched with surprise and some even with entertainment.

 

Gillian only realized what she done when she saw the fiery glaze adorned Ruby's eyes. Oh God, what had she done? She hadn't meant to be so impetuous but Ruby's harsh words had stabbed her too personally that it was hard to control her inveighing anger.

 

Kenny and Takeshi had to rush over to Ruby's side by now. They did not know what really was going on, but naturally, they wavered to Ruby's side and gave Gillian harsh stares. "I can't believe this!" Takeshi shrilled, referring to the mess of the scene and the damage of Ruby's shirt. "Isn't that Prada's?!"

 

"You, you've gone way too far," Kenny spoke up in Ruby's defense. "She made you sit at another table, but that didn't give you the right to insult and attack her."

 

"For real!" Takeshi boomed in echoing effect. "How dare you offend THE Ruby Lin! Do you know who her father is? Hell, do you even know who I am?" Gillian stared at him as though he was born from outer space. "I'm Takeshi Kaneshiro, and I have the biggest integrity! If you hurt my friends, I will never let you get away with it."

 

"Bravo!" Kenny supported Takeshi.

 

Charlene rolled her eyes. "Come on dudes, we're not on T.V."

 

Gillian wasn't going to let people tramp all over her like a mat like that. "Are these your two dogs?" Gillian asked Ruby with mock running her words. Apparently, she wasn't the type to accommodate anyone at any given situation. "You can't even fight your own battle?"

 

Ruby clenched her fists, feeling exasperation thundering down her nerves. "I'll show you what battle is!" With that, she used her claws and grabbed Gillian by the hair, pushing her backwards. They both slammed into the wall with Gillian smashing against it with a loud BAM! The force sent the wall shaking and even some picture frames dismantled from their hooks and fell to the ground with loud shattering sounds.

 

People gasped with terror and shock as they watched the two girls fought like immature imbeciles.

 

Edison couldn't believe the asinine scene. Although he really wasn't on Ruby side and detested her way of handling the matter, he couldn't help but cheer Ruby on with every fiber of his being. "Whoo! Go Ruby!" He rooted, earning a hazardous yet confused stare from Daniel.

 

"Oh my dear lord..." Charlene breathed out as she watched the two scattered the food on the nearby table to the floor. People jumped up from their seats and spat out curses. Chaos enveloped the whole restaurant until the manager finally came out and stopped the two fiery women.

 

"Stop! We will call the police if you two ladies don't!" The bald man's blaring voice pierced through the room.

 

Gillian and Ruby were pried apart by two security guards. Both of them were panting and out of breath; still, their immense revulsion ebbed in the eyes did not falter and remained aflame.

 

Hell, it was bound to stay that way for eternity.
