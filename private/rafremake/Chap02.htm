The Rich & the Fabulous: The Remake

by Bunny Ears

Chapter Two: Busted! Newfound Discovery
-

"Hello, someone needs to tell me where to go," Edison was saying, irritance leaking through his words. "I've been driving for awhile now but I don't even know where the hell I'm heading to." He flicked a glance at Gillian through the rear mirror. Anyone could sense the barefaced dislike he harbored in those luminous eyes.

His indirect scorn made Gillian's skin itch with repulsion. Had she not owe him a favor, she would have fought him verbally.

"Oh, I'm so sorry." Her tone sounded phony, but she wasn't about to stoop that low and actually expend an effort to sound sincere to a motherless bastard like him. "Why didn't you say so earlier? It wasn't my intention to take up your precious time and waste your precious gas."

Daniel, who was oblivious to the rising tension between the duo, intervened. "Hey, what's your name? I believe we haven't had proper introductions."

Her eyes sparked. "The name's Gillian Chung. Yes, I'm sort of new here, so forgive me if I say anything that might offend you, because you know I tend to talk--"

"Too much?" Edison filled in for her with a sneer. He peered at her detestably through the rear mirror. "Well, that's certainly a surprise." Sarcasm.

She flashed a forced smile but in actuality, her fists were clenching in annoyance. "Your turn," she reminded.

"Ed," he replied, composed. His eyes bore straight at the road without a glance at her. "But you see that's what close friends call me. As for you, I'd appreciate it if you just stick to something as polite-sounding as Mr. Chen." There was a short pause, as though he was repeating those words in his head. "Hey, it actually sounds pretty cool. Mr. Chen." He said it with a suave tone, actually liking the authorative sound of the name.

Gillian had to fight the urge to roll her eyes at his ridiculousness and childishness. "Oh, all right then... Chen." The pause was deliberate, in a mock to his. Delight thrummed her veins when she saw his jaw clenched.

"Mr. Chen," he corrected bluntly.

Puzzlement usurped Daniel's face in a way it made him look distorted. "Is it me, or do I feel that you guys don't really like each other?"

How true that was. The fact was, the moment she laid eyes on Edison, Gillian had jumped into rash conclusions that he was a mean asshole with a foul mouth. Furthermore, the way he talked and acted just simply peeved her to no end. Call it hate at first sight. Like in the real world, there are always people that you will like at first glance, but there are also those that you will hate. There are those that you like from the start but will hate in the end. Likewise, those you hate may be the ones you will grow to appreciate.

Edison belonged to the group that she hated from the very start. The chance of she changing her opinion seemed just as improbable as it was impossible.

"And you're Daniel Wu..." Gillian said, avoiding his directed input. "As in the son of Adam Wu?"

There was no hint of surprise that tinted his eyes. "Um, yes," he said with an admirable politeness she had to credit him for. Not many can carry such manners. Ahem, like the one sitting next to him. "You know my dad?"

She felt her muscles tensed uncontrollably as she fought for the courage to whip out an appropriate and reasonable reply. "Of course not. I just heard, that's all."

A flicker of doubt shone in Edison's eyes. "How is that so? Didn't you say you're new here?"

Her reflex was quick. "Well yes, but I just heard about him this morning when I boarded off the plane. Some lady was talking about him. Famous, no?" She gulped fretfully. Even she had to wince at that sorry excuse. "Anyway... Maybe you guys can drop me off at the motel, if that works."

"Motel?" Daniel arched an eyebrow. "Those places aren't very safe for someone like you."

"What a homeless girl to do?" Gillian asked in an innocent tone. "Ahh, if only I could afford to live in the states... I would've stayed..."

Edison rolled his eyes at her fake tone. Too bad Daniel seemed to think otherwise. "Don't worry about it," Daniel assured like a gentleman. "In fact, why don't we help you move into a refined hotel instead? We'll pay for your expenses."

Sure, Edison knew his friend was pretty naive given that he was a rich boy and had never really experienced the horrors of life, not to mention the boundless kindness of his big heart, but there has got to be a limit to a man's stupidity. Edison was beginning to think his friend might be swinging a little crush on the stranger.

Shudder. He thought. Just shudder.

"Whoa, whoa!" Edison exclaimed in dramatic exaggeration that made Gillian roll her eyes. "You want to help her, but don't drag me down the pothole."

"Come on, don't be so petty. She can really use both of our help."

There was a snort of disagreement. Gillian could clearly see the boundary of this man's generosity, and boy if it wasn't shocking. "I'd rather eat my money, than use it to help her. Like, seriously, did I even hear a word of thanks for this trouble I didn't have to be involved in, in the first place?"

Gillian realized it was time to put an input. "Look, guys, I don't hope to bring troubles. I'll be fine stuck in a regular motel. I thank you for your offer, but it's not necessary. I'm not that poor to the state I can't even afford a room. Beside, my aunt lives in Hong Kong. I'm planning to stay over hers after I settle down... a bit."

"Yeah, you heard her," Edison said lazily.

Daniel was still persistent to help. "Okay, fine, if you're so stingy, I'll help her out myself. Pull yourself together and start the damn car."

Gillian would protest but then the more she thought about it, the more the idea appealed to her. Beside, wouldn't it be too rude to reject him even further when he was so sincere and inclined to help? In the end, she graciously accepted.

Edison shook his head to the side. Stupid, mother-effing idiot.

**************

Edison slammed the luggage on the floor once they reached the hotel room, bearing no respect or even an ounce of patience. Hell, he wouldn't even help her carry the luggage in the first place if Daniel hadn't begged him to while he scuttled off somewhere to buy this ungrateful imp some dinner! Hell, Edison wouldn't even succumb to anybody's begging if it weren't for his own suspicion of this little girl that made him want to interrogate her. And he couldn't do that if Daniel were there...

So...

This was the perfect time.

"Are you even a man?" Gillian bore no restraint as she shot him the question, referring to his uncivilized act of throwing her property around as if he had every single right in the whole world to do so.

"Look, it's only me and you here so you can quit your acting, little lady," Edison started. "Don't think you are so smart. My experienced eyes can see through you like an invisible glass. You're here for some hidden agenda, aren't you?"

"What are you talking about?" Gillian hissed and backed away. "Daniel wanted to help me."

He snorted with derision, a gesture she didn't appreciate. "Yeah. First you got run down by a gang of wolves. Then, you know about that Adam Wu thingy, and then the staying at the motel excuse to take advantage of Daniel. Sounds kind of forced, don't you think?"

His condemning tone sliced every inch of her tolerance and drained most of her patience. "Hey, I'm just telling the truth. Can't help it if you don't believe me. Now excuse me, I have to unpack my stuff?"

"Yeah, as if I'm going to believe in your bullshit," Edison spat. It surprised her how mean he was turning into. Initially, she thought he was just an immature boy but he proved to be more than what just meets the eye. "Sorry, I'm not as gullible as Dan is."

"Just what the fuck is your problem?" Out of tactics, Gillian resorted to profanity. He thought he could disrespect her like that? "Ever since we meet, you're just continuously bitching at me and going against everything I do!"

"Yeah, maybe we're destined to be enemies. How cool is that?" said Edison with fake humor. "I'll tell you what my problem is: What I hate most is people lying, especially if that happens to my friends. I don't know what your intention is, but if you're planning to hurt Dan, you're messing with the wrong dude."

She rolled her eyes in irritation. "Is that supposed to be a threat, Chen? Wow, I actually thought you were more than that."

"Oh shut up. You think you're so great?" Edison shot back. "It's Mr. Chen," he corrected.

"You're just as immature and as stupid as a five-year-old, so I do not need to address you with that kind of respect," Gillian said sharply. "Chen."

The usual stubborn Edison wasn't going to succumb that easily. Pride and anger gave way to impulsiveness as he dashed forward where her luggage lay open and grabbed her passport. "You know what; I'm going to send you back to where you come from. Wherever in hell that is." He flipped open her passport and as his eyes read the bold letters of her name, his mind contorted into a world of squiggly lines. "What? Wu Yan Tong? I thought you said your last name was Chung?"

She snatched it back. "You do not intrude people's privacy like that, you moron!"

"I knew it. You, you definitely have some sort of hidden agenda shoved in your sleeves. You know what; I'm not leaving until I find out what your motive is."

His obstinacy sickened her to a degree she could not withstand. "Don't make me get physical," she threatened.

"God, I'm like, so scared," Edison shrieked with sarcasm dripping his words. "Puny girl like you is no match for me, so give it up. What's your damn agenda, huh?"

"None of your concern, okay?"

Edison's mind took a stroll. Wu Yang Tong, where had he heard of such name? The familiarity of the name pounded inside his head as he struggled to fetch for answers. Wu Yang Tong. That was it! Memories came swimming back in only an instant. "Shit! That's impossible! You can't be..."

Gillian's eyes widened in horror at the prospect that he knew. At this rate, she could not run, she could not avoid, and definitely she could not lie. Her nerves shook in anxiety as she searched for something to say. "I..." What else was there to say? The look in his eyes already divulged his newfound discovery.

Edison was torn in words. He had heard numerous references about Wu Yan Tong and was sure that this was no sick coincidence. Yet he couldn't assume it was true until he gets the answer from her mouth. "Are you his sis--"

"So what if I am?" Gillian harshly spat. "It's none of your damn business! So let me tell you! You had better keep your mouth shut or else--"

"Or what? You'll pound me to death?" Edison asked.

"No! I will chop you to death!" She pushed him out of her way. Bad move. Bad bad move. Who ever knew this guy was as weak and rootless as a feather? He crashed into the cabinet behind her with a loud slam! But no, that wasn't it. He had to, he just had to stumble forward, trip over the lifeless luggage and tumble towards her way.

And no, that still wasn't it.

He HAD to choose--well, not really choose- to fall over her. Gillian let out a yelp as time froze for one millisecond as matrix exaggerated and spun around in three dimensional mode before the both of them crashed onto the mattress with an OOOF!

Edison stared into their eyes. They were warped into a world that no longer was reality as they sauntered through each other's eyes. Edison didn't know what he was feeling but god he felt as if he was just immersed into an ocean as he stared at her; waves and waves overlapped as he fell into a deep abyss impossible to climb out. And then, those luscious red lips, the smooth skin, all beckoned his whole attention--

"Get off of me!" Gillian shrieked, snapping him out of his reverie. Edison's eyes regained their normality as he realized what awkward position he was in.

"Crap, my shirt!" Edison cried when he hopped up, only to find that the button of his dress shirt was entangled with her sweater.

Okay, so not only was his button got tangled with her sweater, but it seemed as though it got stuck at the wrong place! "You pervert! Get your hands off of me!"

"Uh yeah, as if I'm not trying!" Edison retorted back with weighty sarcasm as he tried to pry the button and yarn of her sweater apart. But she kept on moving that it was nearly impossible to attempt at untying them. "Can you not move?!" Edison hissed with nil patience.

Suddenly, they heard footsteps. Footsteps approaching their room. The sight of them was almost unmentionable and oh my god, what if it someone saw them this way? Ed's hand all over her-- Oh my God! Gillian couldn't let her image defer like this. She was a girl full of pride and self esteem and she would never let anyone catch her in such an improper position, and not to mention it must be Daniel, her brother!

Gillian pushed Edison away and rolled onto the floor, in the process, tearing her sweater.

Edison looked at Gillian in bafflement before registering what just happened and shoved his hand behind him, where a piece of her sweater still hung onto his button.

"Hey--" Daniel stopped short in his sentence, seeing Edison standing like a scarecrow. "Where's Gillian?"

"Oh, I'm here!" Gillian popped her head up from behind the bed and pulled a bright, but fake smile. "I'm just uh... shoving my luggage under the bed."

Daniel nodded with acknowledgement. "Here, I bought you some food from across the street. Edison and I have to get home now. But if you need help, just feel free to call me anytime," he said, giving her his phone number, in which Gillian insisted that he just put on the night desk. "Come Edison, let's go."

Edison trailed after Daniel. But he didn't leave the room before shooting Gillian a questioning look. Edison didn't know why she didn't tell Daniel that she was his sister. God, never would he imagine that she'd be the little Miss Gillian he'd heard about! Her mother was a tramp, as people said. No wonder her daughter was uncivil and rude, and ungrateful, and just totally intolerable like this!

Edison hopped into the driver's seat as Daniel amounted on the other side. Edison studied Daniel's expression. He was smiling like a complete idiot! What the hell?

"Hey are you feeling like donating some million dollars to the charity?" Edison asked.

Daniel gazed at his friend. "How'd you know?"

"Uh duh! Because a fairy god mother told me!" Edison said sarcastically with a roll of his eyes. "Dude! Don't tell me you're liking a girl whom you didn't even meet for half a day?!" Edison wanted to spill out the beans, but remembering Gillian's threatening eyes made him jump back in consideration. He was always one who had integrity.

So he couldn't spill it out. At least, not yet.

"What are you talking about?" Daniel asked outrageously, putting Edison in relief. Phew. So he really didn't-- "We did know her for half a day!"

Edison slapped his forehead. He had hoped that Daniel was denying it. At least that would be better, but no! It appeared as if Daniel was deeper in the hole than he first thought! "Dude, you can't--"

"Don't tell me you're interested in her?" Daniel shot Edison a pair of killer eyes.

"You have got to be fucking kidding--" Edison paused as a revelation hit him hard and square. Crap! Daniel really liked her! But she was his sister! And he was her brother! That mean.. That mean... if they went out... and got married... and had kids... "What? Of course!" Edison revoked his initial disgust and slapped on a feigned serious face. "She's gorgeous! Let me tell you! I'm courting her! So you--back off!"

Whoa. That certainly came out as a surprise. But Edison kept in mind that he said those things because he wanted to push his friend away from the wrong path and not because he really meant it.

Daniel gazed at Edison in utter disbelief. "I met her first. It made no sense if I'm the one backing off and not you," he stated calmly. "Well, I'm not surprised. Gillian is really gorgeous. I don't blame you if you love her at first sight. Good stuff gets competition you know? So if you like her, then let me tell you, work hard. Because I'm going to snatch her away from you real bad if you don't."

Okay, so maybe she wasn’t that bad, but come on! She was as shrewd as a witch can be! No way will he ever like such a gross girl!

Edison wanted to retort back. Damn, he should have just told Daniel the truth! But now, it was too late. He couldn't let Daniel fall into a tragic doom. No way, no how. Not when he could help it. Even if it meant sacrifice, then so be it, Edison will court that Gillian Chung.

For Daniel's sake, he had to.
