Chapter 4

Rejection? Not Yet, Boy.

Ariel banged her head against the desk. Old Mr. Wang's monotonous voice did nothing but worsen her state. She recalled all of the things happened in the past two days with remorse and humiliation. First of all, running into Mike was a bad blow to her opened wounds. Secondly, that stupid challenge would not leave the premises of her bothered mind. Lastly and most mortifying of all, that Joe Cheng rejected her not once, but *two* times.

Ariel remembered that day clearly when he left her home.

"I'm not going on that trip to the temple, just so you know."

God damn it. God should forbid anyone to speak in such a condescending and arrogant tone! What clearly made it worse was the fact he announced it to her grandpa and his mother as if she was some sort of unwanted, contagious object.

Ariel had enough.

She knew that it was impossible to back out of the challenge. She would prove to her friends and Mike that she was moving on. And so, despite her pride, Ariel would continue to march on this road. Date him then dump him.

Of course she wasn't going to speed onto it without a proper map. Ariel had some hidden plans to ease the hardship the road will offer.

But first of all, somehow, some way, she just had to get him to go on a date with her.

----------------------------------------------------------

It was lunch time. When the bell rang, Ariel immediately sped out of class on the quest to search for Joe. Rainie and Gillian were both alarmed that she was not going to eat lunch with them, but Ariel had no choice. When it came to Joe Cheng, even three months wouldn’t be enough to do anything. Time was a bomb and she had to make every second useful.

Ariel didn't know where Joe would be at this time. Where would someone who hated accompany eat? Hell, did he even eat lunch at all? He was always nonexistent when it came to hanging out time. Ariel won't be surprised if he's hiding in a closet right now.

It took her approximately ten minutes running from one end to another to find Joe in the school library, reading books.

Talk about a big nerd! Ariel giggled mentally as she approached him.

"Hey," she said, slipping into the seat next to him.

Joe was initially alarmed, but when he saw it was Ariel, an annoyed expression scattered on his face as he turned away, pretending not to see her.

Ariel frowned, not liking his reaction. But she tamed her anger down. She shouldn't be too harsh--that might backfire her plans. "So... What are you reading?"

Of course, as she expected, he ignored her again.

Ariel took a peek at the book's title. "Japan and War?" She asked, slightly amused. "So, you like history? You defnitely look like someone who appreciate those stuff."

She thought she saw a smirk. "Yeah, unlike some bonehead."

Ariel flinched. He was not hesitant to insult her at any given situation, even when she was actually complimenting him. Usually, Ariel would blow up and give him a piece of her mind, but for her own sake, she will restrain herself. She mustn't be too aggressive! "Well, what good does reading do you when you don't even know what's filial piety is," she commented coolly with pounds of underlying meanings. "Ahh~You're so handsome, so tall, and so smart. Such a pity that you're cold-blooded and bad."

Ariel watched him. She saw the way his eyes flinched with irritation and smiled, knowing that she must have stricken a chord of some sort. As she expected and hoped for, he turned around and gave her a cold glare. One that could make one's hair all stand up.

"Just stay what you want to say. Stop beating around the bush," he spat.

With a smile of gayety, she scurried to his other side such that her face was nearer towards his. "Go on that temple date with me, please?"

Almost instantly, he uttered, "No."

Ariel plastered on a puppy look, eyes begging. "Please?"

"No."

"Come on. Just a date. Please?"

"No."

"It's not like it's going to be only us two. There's my grandpa, and there's your mom. You should accompany her, no? Come on, please?" She nudged him.

"I said, no!" Joe hissed, goaded at last. His dynamic tone caught several people's attentions and he turned away, slightly embarrassed.

"Why?!" Ariel asked, infuriation simmering inside her blood. His stubbornness was something that she could not understand. It was just a tiny date; it wasn't as if she was asking him to commit murder, so why was he so insistent on rejecting her?

"Because I said so."

Joe began packing his books, declaring his nil tolerance to further the conversation with her. Ariel saw his haste as a contribution to her growing desperation and grabbed his backpack so he couldn't go. "If you're not telling me the real reason why, I'm not going to let go!"

"What do you get out of this?" Joe asked vexingly.

"Not going to tell you unless you tell me your reason."

"Has anyone ever told you you're a pest?"

Ariel stared at him, slightly offended. "Well, has anyone ever told you you're pretty ugly when you're stubborn?"

"Bullshit," he coldly spat, not at all affronted. "Are you going to let go or not?"

"No!" Ariel shook her head.

The annoyance on his face grew more visible by the second. "There's a cockroach by your arm."

"What!" Ariel's eyes widened as she jumped frightfully, the mere word 'cockroach' making her heart thrash a thousand times harder.

Joe immediately took the chance to snatch his backpack from her. With a smirk, he threw it upon his shoulder and gave her a derogatory look, not minding the extra attention he was getting from nearby readers. "You're so scared of something so little when you're that big?" he asked with mock dripping his words. "Stupid baby."

And he walked away, just like that, leaving Ariel widened-eyed and sputtering with disbelief.

Strike three!

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Two more days.

Her dateline was just two more days. Ariel's mind was exploding with thoughts of how to force Joe to comply to her date. He had a polished reputation of not wavering his decisions once he made his mind, but the determined Ariel knew that no matter how confident a person was, he must have a weakness. She was planning to channel on that weakness--whatever it was--and make him agree to their date!

Ariel flinched. She had been so crazy with the bet these days she wasn't sure if she was herself anymore. However, the image of Mike fondling with his girlfriend would constantly haunt and taunt her mind, successfully igniting her will to harden her urge for revenge. She just couldn't take how he was laughing at her behind her back. She must counterattack; there was no other choice.

"Exams are just around the corner," Mr. Wang announced to the disinterested class, shuffling a stack of papers in his grip. "I expect that everyone study hard and thrive for a good score." Here, he specifically aimed a glance at Ariel, who only shrugged off slightly and returned to her train of thoughts.

"However, I am aware that many of my students are lazy when it comes to studying," the old man drone on monotonously. "Therefore I've constucted a plan to pair you up with a study buddy. You must aid each other on the preparation worksheets. I expect effort and good results. Understand?"

There was an occasional groan here and there but it seemed like everybody was fine with Mr. Wang's wacky antics. Silence enveloped the room as the old man began to recite names.

"...Joe and Ariel."

Shooting her head up, Ariel turned to glance furtively at Joe, who remained indifferent to whatever that was surrounding him. Normally, Ariel would never turn her eyesight back there, for nothing held her interest. However, ever since the deal to pursue Joe, Ariel found herself stealing glances at his direction and paying more attention to him than she had ever done before.

Initially, Ariel didn't like the idea of working with a high-class arrogant jerk, but the second she realized it was a golden opportunity, Ariel swapped on a pleased smile. She didn't care what he was thinking at the moment about their partnership, but she was sure as hell elated that she was given this rare chance to get close to him. God was helping her. She definitely can make him comply to their date two days later...

"Any questions? If not, then you are dismissed."

The bell rang right after Mr. Wang finished his sentence. Mobs of bodies began swarming out of the door.

Ariel quickly caught up to Joe, smiling in mischief. "Hey, *partner*!"

She saw the way his eyes cast down at her in a degrading manner, but tried her best to suppress the urge to kick him. "What?" he said.

"Didn't you listen? We're study buddies! Say, why don't we start today?"

He rolled his eyes. "I have something important to do."

"Come on"! Ariel insisted, grabbing onto his arm in a friendly manner. "What can be more important than studying for exams?"

He stopped in his tracks, studying her suspiciously. "Surprise, surprise. The incompetent student wants to study. What else do you have up in your sleeves?"

Ariel quickly frowned at his insensitive tone. He never ceased to supply her with hurtful adjectives; however, upon remembering her mission, Ariel was willing to push aside all grudges and be generous for the sake of victory. "Listen, I am hardly ever determined to study for exams. You have better not crush my good intention to help you!"

Joe rose an eyebrow, peering down at her further. He made a small movement of inching closer to her face. "Help me? It's more like the opposite."

"What does that supposed to mean, you dolt?!" Ariel shot fiercely, stepping back to escape the close proximity of his body. "I am not as incompetent as you think! Don't you know your..." Ariel couldn't find a subject to fill in the blank. She was startled at the possibility that he might as well was good in everything damn subject. "--your... handwriting needs a lot of work!" she supplied lamely. "The examiners probably can't even read your essay! You just remember these things when you get back your score! Hmph!"

Joe rolled his eyes. "Bad handwriting doesn't necessarily mean I'll fail the test. As long as it's legible, I don't see a problem."

"Well..." Ariel was racking her mind for something smart to say. "Umm.--"

"Just admit that you want my help," Joe filled in, smirking.

"What?" Ariel widened her eyes. Pfft! That was far from her intention. She never studied in her whole entire life and still managed to pass the class with credible marks. She did not need his help!

Seeing that she was stumped, Joe continued, "Look, there's no need for us to cooperate. I see no benefit to help you. In fact, you might delay my studies even further."

Ariel couldn't stand the way he spoke of contempt and scorn. Just because he was smarter than her didn't mean he had the right to speak so highly of himself. She had her own pride and dignity, too. "Wei! What's wrong with you? Don't you know that it is assigned that we *must* study together? If I go and tell Mr. Wang of your non-cooperative attitude, what will he say? I'm not just doing this for my sake. It's for your own good too."

"Are you done?" asked Joe cooly, still disinclined to comply.

For Ariel, this was the last straw. "No!" she yelled, inching closer to him. "I can't believe you have all those admirers that you have! You're heartless! They can't see it but I do! Have you ever thought that you're not the most incredible thing to grace this earth? That there is more than just brains and looks?! You might get by for a little while, but your cold attitude isn't going to get you very far in the real world, *Prince*."

There was a slight moment of silence as Ariel witnessed the softening of his eyes. Did she touch a soft spot?

"You're just yapping all this bullshit because you want to study right?" He asked, without glancing down at her. "Fine. We'll meet in the library fifteen minutes from now."

And with that, he trode away, leaving Ariel completely surprised.

----------------------------------------------------------

"Here, you read that, and I'll read this. After we're done, we'll exchange analysis."

Ariel stared down at the book Joe just handed her. It was thick, almost two inches. What was he thinking? "Wei, you're not serious, are you? How in the world are we going to finish these books?!"

"Who said we have to finish it?" Joe said in that same dergatory tone. "Don't you know how to skim and pick up the main points?"

"Hah, you might as well just read the summary!" Ariel snapped.

Joe rolled his eyes. "Summaries aren't detailed enough. You need to delve in deeper. Minor details can also be important."

"This is foreign language," Ariel pointed out the obvious. "Not literature class. We just need to get the mechanics right."

"Is that how you view it?" Joe asked incredulously. "Look, you have to write an essay in English. You have to analyze things. You're not going to pass if you just study the mechanics--something you should have already known, anyway."

Ariel had to try her best to restrain from cracking his jaw open. All throughout the session, he continuously hinted that she was dumb. He was always the one commanding her to do this and that, never once asking her how she thought or felt toward the subject. There was some things that she couldn't even understand yet he made no effort to clear it for her; instead, he continued on and on, expecting that she understand every word.

"I quit!" She announced heatedly. "Who cares about the essay part? As long as I do good in the other sections, I'll pass!"

"So, that's it? Your determination is that thin?" Joe asked. "Don't you want to improve your scores?"

Ariel thought for a moment. Not really. The actual reason that she even bothered to study with him was because she wanted to get close to him. She had not neglected her mission to ask him out on the date. However, for the past hour or so, her brain was tortured with so many foreign material that it almost lost its normal function. Now that he reminded her, Ariel decided to shove aside all grievances and swapped on a smile. "Hey, Joe. Have you considered my proposition?"

He looked rather confused. "What proposition?"

"I meant the date!" Ariel said with a hopeful face.

Joe's expression turned grim all of a sudden. "No."

“You!”

Ariel stopped in puzzlement. She turned around and saw a beautiful girl walking towards them with a smug look on her face. Her heels clicked against the marble floor, her stride a marveling sophistication only found on super models.

"You're talking to me?" Ariel asked, slightly stunned at the fact someone unfamiliar was talking to her.

The girl flicked her hair. For a moment, Ariel couldn't stop thinking how beautiful she looked when she did that. Undoubtedly, she must have a line of guys chasing after her. With that kind of beauty, it was hard not to have that kind of popularity. "You're Ariel Lin Yi Chen aren't you, the girl who got dumped by her boyfriend and goes after boys?." It was more of a statement than a friendly question.

Ariel nodded, still baffled as ever. "Do I know you?" 'Wow, I didn't know I was that popular and well known!' she thought. But then, the way the girl said it sounded malicious, if not acidic.

Wait a minute, ‘goes after boys?’ Where in the world did that idea come from?

The girl approached closer towards her, her strong fragrance attacking Ariel's nose. Only girls like her could pull off that kind of stunt without being laughed at. "What does someone like you have to do with the cold prince?” she asked in a degrading tone while trying to stay sweet and endearing to Joe. “Joe, you shouldn’t degrade yourself by sitting with her. Why don’t we go over there?”

Ariel blinked. "Excuse me?" she asked.

"Take a look at yourself. Fine, I'll be kind today. Take my advice, little girl. You're so ugly. You should give up and save yourself from embarrassment."

Exasperation immediately thundered down Ariel's nerves. "Wei, who do you think you are?"

"Oh, you don't know?" The girl asked. "I'm Jolin Tsai. Well, I don't blame you. You seem like that type who's too stupid to take notice of these things anyway. I don't mind. Just make sure you don't forget my name from today onwards, bitch."

"Watch your words," Ariel grumbled, returning an equally fierce glare. "Why would a princess like you be in the library anyway? Shouldn't you be at the malls or something?"

Jolin smirked. "Oh, I heard that Joe's here, so I just wanted to pay him a visit. I didn't expect that someone like you would be here too..."

"Oh? Do I sense jealously?" Ariel batted her eyelashes. "Ah, sorry that I snatched your man. How about next weekend? I'll make sure he's free."

The clenching of Jolin's jaw was audibly obvious, however, she still bore the same sweet grin. "Don't get cocky, bitch. You're just lucky you're paired up with Joe." Realizing that she was paying more attention to a bitch than to her precious crush, Jolin quickly diverted her attention. "So Joe, are you busy after this? I was hoping that we can hang out..."

"I'm busy."

"But--" Jolin wanted to protest in that sickeningly high voice of hers.

"Can't you see?" Ariel interrupted, "He's a busy man. Got no time for you."

"You..." Jolin looked like she wanted to pull out Ariel's hair as her nostrils flared. However, she ignored the signs Joe was sending her, and plopped on the seat next to his, purposely scooting closer to him. The way she teased his hair was an inconspicuous method of flirtation that made Ariel want to vomit badly. "How about this weekend? My family's holding a barbeque. It would be so much fun if we both can hang out together at my house..."

Joe ignored her, his eyes glued to the book.

Ariel snifled a snicker, but Jolin caught it. Embarrassment further reinforced her attempt to possess Joe's attention. "Joe! I am more interesting than that stupid book!" She yanked his arm in a force that sent Joe out of his concentration. "Look at me!"

Jeez, Ariel thought. Talk about desperate!

"Listen, Jolin. I'm busy this weekend," Joe repeated agitatedly.

"With what?" Jolin stubbornly probed.

"It's none of your business," Joe snapped.

"I must know!" She insisted. Then she turned her attention to Ariel and gasped in horror. "What, don't tell me you're spending your weekend with her?"

Ariel was about to rebuke when what Joe did next completely threw her back. "Yes. We have a date."

"Whs-what?" Jolin sputtered, disbelief suffusing her cheeks. "That is impossible! She's... she's one ugly bitch! You cannot go out with her!!!"

That brat! Ariel seethed inside mentally, glaring at Jolin with revulsion so great it could reach the sky. Ariel began to wonder what sort of relationship Jolin had with Joe. Could they be ex-lovers? But then again, Ariel had never heard about him going out with anyone, so that wasn't possible. The only other answer she could come up with was that Jolin was just another secret admirer.

Ariel could tell from the way Joe's flinched that he wanted her claws off of him. Ariel smirked, deciding to give him a hand. "Oh, come on, Jolin. How low can you stoop? He's already rejected you. Save the last piece of your dignity and take the hint to leave."

Jolin glared at Ariel and then turned back to Joe, expecting that he'd speak up in her defense. Much to her chagrin, he did none of that. Completely defeated, Jolin stomped off in sass. Even the way she swayed her hips looked majestic.

Ariel wanted to burst out laughing when she caught Joe's grim expression. "Hey, good one," she complimented. "You are holding true to your words right? You admitted that we have a date. You cannot back out because I'm sure Jolin will be chasing your heels. Okay, so it's settled?"

Joe didn't answer. He only gave her an annoyed look.

Ariel laughed hard inside. Who ever thought that this was so easy?
