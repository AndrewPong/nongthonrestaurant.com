Chapter 13

Convo Gone... Wacko?

"I can't believe it... Just when I thought I fell in love!" Rainie bawled as she grabbed tissues and tissues to blow her running nose. She could not help but cry continuously at the painful disappointment.

Ariel continued to feed her friend tissues. Gillian was trying to best to soothe the girl's emotions by patting her but everything was to no avail. The girl was too heartbroken and could only spill her tears to demonstrate her misfortune.

"Rainie, it's not your fault," Ariel tried to assure her. "You didn't know he was that type of guy. Believe me, it's his loss."

"I can't believe I was fooled!" Rainie cried. "He was so nice!"

Ariel pulled her friend into a hug. "All guys are jerks," she spat with abhorrence, remembering Mike. "They consider us girls as objects to attain, but once they do that, they'll go seek for another girl. They're better off dead than wasting space on this planet." Her words filled with immense hatred for the opposite sex, something that would be considered weird to come out from a girl who currently has a boyfriend, but Rainie was far too heartbroken to look into her words.

Rainie bawled again, wailing like a little girl. "I thought I'd be fine, but it hurts too much! I really did genuinely like him, even though it was only two days!"

Ariel nodded, showing her understanding. Actually, she was a bit surprised at how Rainie reacted to this relationship. Perhaps, it was first love despite not being her first relationship. Rainie never seemed like the type to cry over guys like this, but maybe love was that powerful...

Ariel should know. She had gone through the same thing with Mike for three months. Now, it was hard for her to regain that innocent trust in guys. Seeing Rainie going through what she had, Ariel was exploding with uptight sympathy and sorrow.

"Rainie, don't worry. You'll find a better guy than him," Gillian cajoled.

Ariel tightened her hug on the shivering girl. How could love make one smile with priceless bliss yet at the same time cry one's heart out?

The next day, Ariel was exhausted. Yesterday, Rainie wanted to stay over at her home and both girls talked and accompanied each other throughout the night without sleep. Her eyes were now surrounded by black bags and she looked horrible as ever.

Thank God the day had already ended. Ariel didn't know how she survived but she was damn glad that she did.

Suddenly, she bumped into someone. It took her a few seconds or so to realize it, but when she did, there wasn't even enough energy to appear surprised.

"You're like a walking zombie," Joe commented, studying her appearance with disgust. "Are you really going to study like this?"

Ariel gazed at him, a bit puzzled. "Study?"

"What have you been doing all night? Playing video games perhaps?" Joe derogatorily asked. "You forgot the exam's tomorrow?"

He was getting impatient but Ariel finally remembered. The day they'd been preparing and studying for was finally coming. Ariel had told Joe that she was going to pass with high scores this time and she even boasted and told him she was going to treat him to an extravanous dinner but now, how in the world will she prep for the exam like this?

An exhausted sigh flew out of Ariel's mouth as her eyes shot down with little spirits. "So tired. I want to go home and sleep," she said, brushing pass him.

But Joe would not allow that. He grabbed the back of her shirt and pulled her back as though she was a limbless puppet. "Wei, are you planning to fail or something? How will I face Mr. Wang?"

"Sleep. I want sleep," Ariel murmured as though she was in a daze.

"Where did your ambition fly off to? Your determination is that thin?" Joe let out an irritated sigh. But as his will have it, he would not relent that easily. With a strong and ungentle force, he dragged the half conscious zombie across the school's courtyard and out of the door with weird gawks and jealous stares from other students

When Joe successfully dragged Ariel to her home, he rang the door bell. Ariel's mother, Rilla, was surprised to see a handsome young man at her door.

"Hi Rilla," Ariel greeted her mom still in a daze and looking exhausted.

Joe was slightly taken aback by how Ariel referred to her mom with her first name. That was some lack of manners, he thought, but in another aspect, he sort of envied the obvious intimacy. "Hi Aunty."

Rilla's eyes sparked. "Hi there, Joe."

The two stepped into their house with Ariel stumbling across the threshold. "I don't understand why you'd want to come here," she muttered. "Well help yourself, I'm going to sleep."

Joe hissed a "Wei" when she strutted off the stairs, attempting to make a grab for her shirt again but not being so fast this time, he inevitably missed. When he caught Rilla's stare, he flushed with embarrassment.

“Are you helping Ariel study?” Rilla asked, smiling.

Joe nodded uncomfortably. The lady’s eyes were all over him.

"Joe!" Suddenly, Grandfather Lin appeared out of nowhere. "What a pleasant surprise you're here! My god, why didn't you notify us?"

"Hi Grandpa," Joe said politely. "I'm going to go upstairs, if you don't mind."

"Oh no, of course I won't. Just don't be too crazy okay?"

Joe blinked with confusion, but he didn't make a further comment before ascending to Ariel's room. Much to his irritation, the ugly sight of Ariel slouching halfway on her bed greeted him. Apparently, she didn't seem to mind her image.

"Wei, get up," Joe tugged on her arm.

"Leave me alone," she muttered.

"You disappoint me, airhead. I thought you were more than that. But now, it seems that I was wrong. You're just a sloth who obviously has no goal in life."

That moment, Ariel's eyes snapped right open like a revived corpse. His denigrating tone was the main catalyst to her sudden awareness. "Wei, what did you say?!" She hissed, pointing a finger at him with a challenging tone.

"What? Becoming deaf again? You have a weird habit of turning deaf to the things you hate to hear, don't you?" Joe said tauntingly.

"I'm not a sloth!" Ariel bellowed, tempted to be angry.

"You're discarding your studying aren't you?"

"Hahaha, mister, who do you think you are to dictate what I'm doing!" Ariel immediately got up and scurried over to her desk. "See, I'm studying," she said, pulling out books.

Joe smirked. He looked around for an extra chair but there was none. "Wei, is there a chair somewhere for me to sit?"

Ariel grinned, remembering the exact scenario from before. "What? You can always sit on my bed."

"Who wants to sit on your dirty bed?" He instantly retorted.

"There's always the floor." She said, in a mocking tone.

For the next twenty minutes, Ariel ignored his presence. Despite having her eyes glued to the textbook, her mind was anywhere but there. She was actually sleeping, with her eyes open if that was possible. Even if it wasn't, she was at least half unconscious. Her immobility seemed to raise suspicions, unfortunately, and Joe found himself towering over her with the discovery that she was still on the same page as twenty minutes ago.

Joe's patience evaporated instantly. "Ariel Lin Yi Chen!!

Ariel snapped back immediately. It wasn't a everyday thing that he called her by her name, let alone, her full name.

"Is this how you do things?!" Joe blared, no longer controlling his anger. "I took my time to make sure you study and this is how you perform? How do you answer to me, huh?"

Ariel blinked, never seeing him so serious and maddened before. It suddenly made her wonder why he chipped in so much effort when it was her grades, not his. And since when did he care so much about her?

"Forget it. For a lazy pig like you, it'll take a miracle for you to read even a page!" He slammed the textbook shut, frightening Ariel. No longer could she detect the Joe that once tried to protect her. "I'm going home now. Good-bye."

"Wait," Ariel beckoned, feeling slightly guilty. "You're... you're not going to help me study?" Somehow the idea of him leaving didn't sound too pleasant.

"That's just wasting my time," Joe spat.

Ariel turned a bit red. "What's that supposed to mean?"

"It means you're an incorrigible pig," he rebuked, ready to jerk open the door.

Ariel shot up from her seat and approached him, expression a rambling mess of exasperation. Was it her fault that she was this tired? She couldn't help it! Why couldn't he bear her some thought and consideration? If only he knew how hard it was to function twenty four hours without a second of sleep! "You're not allowed to insult me!" Ariel heaved.

"I'd spit some more insults if I had to!" Joe responded.

"You!" Her breath became shallow as she glared daggers at him, feeling her blood pressure shooting up. "Fine! Go! Leave!"

"Gladly."

Joe threw the door open, not bothering to hide his emotions. He was, however, a bit taken aback to find Grandfather Lin and Rilla standing right before him with baffled and questioning expressions.

"You're leaving already?" Grandfather Lin asked with a tinge of dread in his tone. "But you just got here! Stay longer, son!"

"Grandpa! Don't stop him if he wants to go!" Ariel shouted from inside.

"You can't leave so soon. Who is going to help our Ariel study for the exam tomorrow?" Rilla asked with an exaggerated expression. She had heard their argument just right outside the door moments ago. Knowing that Ariel won't study if he left, Rilla was determined to not let Joe leave. But of course, she too wanted them to make-up. Rilla had a sharp eye for men and from the moment she saw Joe, she knew he was the perfect candidate for her sassy daughter even though she knew this relationship was bogus. "Come on, stay. I even make some snacks."

"Sorry Aunty, but I really have to go." Joe attempted to pass the two adults, but like siamese twins, they stuck together, disinclined to let him through.

"You leaving right now means you bear this old man no face," Grandfather Lin said with a grumbling tone. "Young man, have you no respect?"

"Grandpa! Rilla!" Ariel shrieked, appearing at the door. "I don't want him here!" When she met Joe's gaze, her infuriation only escalated by a thousand levels. Go!" She pushed Joe and he slammed into the two adults.

"Wei!" Joe hissed.

"Leave!" Ariel yelled.

Grandfather Lin and Rilla exchanged glances, pushing Joe back into the room with as much force. "You can't leave!" Both adults stubbornly exclaimed. The poor boy was sandwiched between the Lins. It was only in due time 'til he turn into a piece of flattened pancake.

Finally, Joe had enough. The incessant shoving was not only a damage to his physical well being but also to his dying tolerance. "STOP!"

As though he had just pressed a button, the three stopped.

"I'll stay."

Grandfather Lin and Rilla jumped up and down, giving each other high fives while Ariel looked like she wanted to choke Joe. "That's great!" Grandfather Lin exclaimed, shoving Joe back into the room. He smashed into Ariel, who was stubborn as ever to stay rooted to the floor, now allowing him to enter her room.

"Ariel! Get out of the way!" Rilla chided, shoving her daughter to the side. She stumbled a bit and the two adults escorted Joe into the room as though he was a king.

"Have fun studying okay? Here are some cookies and refreshments if you're hungry," Rilla offered a bright gleaming smile before she pulled the grinning grandpa out of the room.

Ariel crossed her arms, not believing her family's childishness and insistence. She didn't understand why practically every adult wanted to matchmake her to Joe. Why was her fate ever intertwined with him? Ever since she met him, disaster continuously came knocking at her door.

"Why did you choose to stay," Ariel asked flatly, still bearing that angry expression.

"Pointless question. Would I have the life to leave if I didn't abide your family's request?"

Silence permeated the air as the two fell into a stale awkwardness. Ariel did not know what to say, but she as hell didn't want to talk to him until he apologized for his crude insults. But then, she didn't expect the silence to be SO awkward.

"Why don't you continue studying," Joe suggested.

Ariel gazed at him, almost in disbelief. He was acting as if nothing had happened and that she could not accept. "What the hell is wrong with you? Don't you even have a least bit of decency to say sorry to me?"

"What mistake did I make? Why do I have to apologize?" He asked obstinately.

"You called me names!" She pointed out loudly and righteously. "If you happened to have forgotten, Mr. Cheng, you clearly agreed to never call me names. I have proof right here." Ariel dug through her drawers and pulled out the contract, pointing at term #3 proudly and arrogantly. "If you don't apologize right now, you'd have to kiss me."

Irritancy swallowed his face whole as he glared at her. It took him several seconds, almost a minute even, to finally utter a "Sorry."

Although it didn't sound sincere enough, Ariel knew she shouldn't push it since she was the one who fell asleep. Not wanting to pursue the matter anymore, she sat at her desk and resumed her studies, already full awake by the earlier commotion. The room was quiet, almost deafening, and after another twenty minutes, Ariel was getting bored.

"Do you want to eat?" Ariel asked, gobbling on a cookie.

Joe shook his head. "I don't like sweets."

"Oh."

He appeared bored, and Ariel bit her lips, guilt rambling inside her. "Look, if you're bored, you can go now. I don't think they will stop you again this time." She said, not looking at him.

"I'm not bored."

"You're a poor liar. Your face says it all."

"Haven’t I always appeared like this?” He asked softly, in deep thought.

“Hmmm. You’re right--” Ariel stopped short when she gazed at him. That was when she caught him in his contemplating expression, something she had never seen before. For a moment, he appeared so withdrawn, almost mysterious.

"Was your family always this lively?"

Huh? Where did that come from? Ariel couldn't help but wonder. "Well, they're a tad bit crazy, but they're still normal people. Don't think they're like clowns or something okay."

"I envy you. Your family seems to care so much about you."

She was beginning to see some bits of his thoughts. "So does your mom," she pointed out, remembering how friendly and welcoming Mrs. Cheng was.

"Really," he said bitterly. "You call ignoring my feelings caring about me?"

Ariel was a bit confused. "What do you mean?"

"Ten years ago, my parents divorced. I wanted to go with dad, but I ended up with my mom. She never asked me who I wanted to go with, only assumed that I needed her, since I was quite young. I was disappointed that dad let me go. He said that it was the best for me, but if there is anyone who understood a bit of me, it would be him. At least, he asks me questions. Unlike my mom, who always assumes. Sure, she provides me a lot, money, clothes, attention, but really, she doesn't even care about my feelings at all. For some weird reason, I never looked up to her as a child should."

Ariel gazed at him; quite taken aback that he viewed his mother in such imperfection. From her encounter with Mrs. Cheng, the lady appeared to be benevolent, almost like a perfect mother. But then, she remembered that night in the forest at the temple, when he once told her that his mother disregarded his feelings and sent him to a new school without his liking. "Why don't you ever talk to her about your problems? From what I see, you lack communication. How is she supposed to know what you're thinking?"

"Shouldn't a mom know what her child wants? And besides, does she ever ask?"

Ariel crawled to the floor and sat next to him against the wall. "Mrs. Cheng probably thought she does know you, so that's why she never asks. The bottom line is, her perception is wrong, and you didn't correct that. You should talk to her. Mothers aren't telepathists."

"She should have at least tried to be."

"Why didn't you tell her that you wanted to go with your dad?" Ariel asked.

"I'd be a burden to him," Joe answered softly. "He was bankrupted at that time, so I would have no chance of staying with him anyway. But still, I've always wondered how I would turn out if I stayed with him instead."

Ariel could see Joe bore a deep admiration for his father, something she found very touching. It was ironic how he loved someone who was far away more than the person who stood next to him all of those years, but then she could see his stance. Although Mrs. Cheng was a nice woman, she lacked that charm to draw her close ones towards her. Maybe it was because as Joe said, she never genuinely cared enough to ask. When a person is disappointed at his mother at a young age, that feeling is sure to shadow the years of his growth, and somehow Ariel could understand that.

"You like your father a lot, don't you?"

Joe gazed at her, sort of smiling in the eyes. "Of course. He is the best. He taught me a lot of things when I was little. Do you know that my first time playing basketball was with him? I was a short kid back then. Who knows, maybe that helped me achieve my height today." He chuckled, making Ariel a bit surprised.

He suddenly realized her intent eyes on him and pulled back his stern expression, knowing that he had spilled too much about him already. "What about you? How come I never see your father around?"

"My father? Oh, he died when I was little." Ariel answered.

"Oh..." Joe did not know how to respond.

"Yeah..." Ariel felt uncomfortable. "Anyway, come on, help me study. I have to get those high scores this time!"

"All of the sudden, you're ambitious again?"

"What? I have always been ambitious!" Ariel retorted. "It's just that yesterday I had no sleep trying to pacify Rainie and her broken heart. Do you have any idea how hard it was for me to finally convince her to go home the next morning?" she sighed, "I'm all used up."

Revelations seemed to occupy Joe. He hadn't seen Rainie at all today so what she said could be true. "You weren't staying up to play video games?"

"Please!" Ariel said, "Who the hell plays games all night long? Might end up getting a seizure or something."

Joe suddenly felt guilty. Earlier, he had accused her of so many things, thinking that she was a lazy sloth who only knows how to play games. But now, he knew his thoughts of her were wrong and that she was just merely a good loyal friend. "I had no idea what a friend you are," he said, suddenly finding her very attractive.

"What, jealous?" Ariel grinned. "Don't worry, when you're heartbroken, I'd be there for you too."

Joe looked at her serious expression before bursting into guffaws. "Has anyone ever told you you're very cheesy?"

Ariel's mouth dropped as she gazed at him with disbelief. "Me? Cheesy? W-w-what are you talking about? Let me tell you!" She scooted closer to him, her face towards his. She was so uptight she didn't notice. "I'm a very cool person! It's just occasionally those uhh... 'cheesy' things slip, but they're heartfelt okay! I can't believe you made fun of me when I'm being very serious. Do you know how that makes me feel--"

Joe, by now, wasn't even listening to a word she was saying. All he could pay attention to was the lips that were moving in fast motion, tempting him with its radiant glow and physical tenderness. He just noticed how full they were, and how peachy. He was sure she wasn't the type to use any sort of lip balm or gloss, but somehow, they were shiny as though she had. What was she blabbering about? Would she just shut up? He thought, eyes never leaving her lips.

Suddenly, he moved in, instantly terminating all distance between their lips. Ariel's sentence got cut off as bewilderment thundered through her body like an electric shock.
