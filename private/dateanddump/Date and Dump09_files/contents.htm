Chapter 9

Live the Drama, Babe.

"I can't believe I agreed to do this," Joe grumbled from behind.

Ariel whipped on her heels in time to see the displeasure written on his face. True, she was still surprised at how he could just impulsively claim to be her boyfriend in front of Mike and Hebe. She was outrageously furious at first, but considering it now, she knew she owed him her gratitude for the help.

"Come on. Pick up your speed!" Ariel gestured for him to hasten. "Rainie and Gillian are waiting."

"I don't get why we have to put up a show for them." Joe's eyes dulled with disinterest. "Can't you just tell them we're together?"

"No," Ariel supplied. "Rainie will not believe unless she sees it with her own eyes."

"This is ridiculous," Joe said. "You girls really have nothing else to do."

Ariel paused as she positioned her hands on her hips, staring at Joe with an irritated look. "Are you telling me that you want to back out now? Remember, I didn't force you to do this. You voluntarily claimed me as your girlfriend!"

He rolled his eyes. "I know. An act I horribly regret."

Even though he didn't say it out loud, Ariel could discern the embarrassment he was feeling. The fact that he was so against the idea of going out with her was definitely stabbing, to say the least. "I will not tolerate your blunt attitude. If you have nothing nice to say, I suggest you keep your mouth shut. No matter how much you are displeased, we are still going to carry out our plans."

Joe reluctantly obeyed, much to Ariel's surprise. Maybe she was thinking too much, but did she see a glimmer of guilt in his eyes? Did he agree out of a obligatory desire to help? He did insult her pretty badly after all without a good reason. It wasn't too much to ask that he rectify the situation that needed rectification.

Rainie and Gillian were practically dropping their jaws when Ariel made her grand entrance to the cafeteria with the cold prince. She could feel the simmering satisfaction as they trode over to the fourth table where her friends were stiffly perched in their seats.

"You can't be serious," Rainie murmured in absolute shock. She managed to pinch herself a couple of times to assure this was no nightmare.

Gillian tilted her glasses like always, studying with a keen eye. But no matter how smart she was, Ariel knew that Gillian wouldn't be able to figure out sentimental matters even if her life depended on it. "This is definitely shocking, but I am happy for you, Ariel."

Rainie's eyes bulged. "How about me?! This means I lose! L-O-S-E!"

Gillian stifled a giggle. "Rainie... You should have known that losing is part of the game."

"I know! But I was SO sure that Ariel will lose!" Now, Rainie was just a panicky mess. For a split second, Ariel almost felt sorry for her friend but quickly suppressed the feeling. What was the point of all her suffering and endless frets if she couldn't see this outcome?

A smirk swept Ariel's face. "Rainie, Rainie, who is the master of all bets now?"

"Please! Our dateline isn't over yet!" Rainie protested. "Let's see if you guys last..."

Now, it was Ariel's turn to be surprised. "Wait, what? You mean we have to drag this on for two months in a half?"

Rainie's trademark grin surfaced. "Of course. You didn't think it was so easy, did you? For all I know, you two can be acting right now. I will NOT lose until you two prove that you are truly in love."

Ariel and Joe exchanged glances. Joe looked unpleasantly disgruntled.

"This is unfair," Ariel voiced out heatedly. "You only specify that we need to date. There isn't any rule that says sentimental feelings be involved. You are ridiculously expecting too much!"

"Fine!!!" Rainie too knew that was a bit far-fetched. "But you two must surpass the dateline in order for me to be convinced that you're truly dating."

Ariel rolled her eyes. "Fine. That'll be easy."

Rainie snickered. "Not unless if you two are faking it."

Ariel was quick to defend. "Of course not! I'll show you that we are a golden couple. Not only will we make it through the next two months, we might surpass SIX months and beyond that!!"

This earned an incredulous look from Joe who looked like he wanted to strangle her until she dropped dead at her feet. Ariel secretly gave him a nudge from under the table for him to play along.

Gillian looked ultimately surprised. "Wow, I never knew you were so ambitious. You two must really be serious with each other."

"Of course," Ariel winked to Joe.

He grumbled more.

"Why doesn't your boyfriend say anything?" Rainie suspiciously asked. "Yo!"

Joe rolled his eyes. "Is there anything for me to say? I'm not interested in your girl talk."

Rainie was quite taken back by his cold attitude. "Wei!" she banged her hand against the table. "I am after all your girlfriend's friend!"

Ariel quickly intervened, seeing that Joe was too annoyed to reply. "Relax, Rainie. You know how he is. He isn't crowned as the cold prince for nothing."

At this, Rainie retreated as she plunged in deep thought. "You're right..." she said, casting a glance at Joe. "Well, that's okay. I'm a very generous person." Her eyes never left Joe when she began talking to him. Sure, she heard a lot about the school's magnet, but to closely view him was actually quite an interesting experience. "You're too lucky, Ariel. Thanks to me, you got your chance to snatch your prince in shining armor. I'm sure Mike will be jealous as hell..."

At the mention of her ex, Ariel felt her stomach do the funny flip. She gulped. Even though she successfully got her revenge, a feeling of unease had always settled inside her...

"Speaking of him, why isn't he coming?" Rainie mused out loud.

Ariel's eyes shot wide open. "What? You invited him?!"

"Of course!" Her friend replied enthusiastically. "As your best friend, I took the liberty to invite him over so that he can see how much you are better off without him! Isn't that what you want?"

Too bad Rainie didn't consider the possibility that perhaps Mike had already known... AND seen them together already. Ariel hadn't notify her friends of the meeting yet. Now it was too late. Even if she said it out loud, there was no way change things now.

Ariel slapped a hand to her foreward, and groaned inwardly. Meeting Mike for the second time only meant more awkwardness and discomfort.

"What's the matter?" Gillian asked. "Are you still uncomfortable to see him?"

"Don't worry!" Rainie interjected. "I'm sure he's the one that will be burning in unease. Ha-ha!"

"I'm leaving." All eyes darted to Joe, who had been silent for awhile. He clearly looked bothered and everyone could see how much his muscles were budging to make the leave. "I can't believe you girls are shallow enough to come up with all these plans just for a guy."

Rainie arched her eyebrows. Joe's cantankerous attitude didn't go unnoticed by her sharp scrutinizing eyes. It was true that he may be an ice person, but was this the way one act toward his girlfriend? If she wasn't wrong, Joe didn't even seem a bit happy to be here.

"Wei! What do you think you're doing?" Rainie snapped coarsely. Her exaggerated actions earned a few looks from their neighbors and Ariel felt embarrassment crawling to burn her cheeks. "How can you leave your girlfriend like that? Are you not a man?"

Joe cast a glance at Ariel. "I figure I did enough for you last time and I've tried my best to tolerate you girl's stupidity. There is no need to carry on. They're your friends so they should understand." He was getting ready to leave. "Unlike you all, I have better things to do."

Rainie stared in utter surprise. "Wait... You mean that you two were really faking it?"

Ariel's face was flushing. She couldn't stand how he could publicly announce it out loud. It was no difference from humiliating her! Pride wouldn't let her keep her mouth shut. "If you were so appalled at this arrangement, maybe you could have considered the choice of telling me earlier! I don't need you to pity me! I don't need your help!"

"You said so," Joe stared coldly.

"So it was all fake, wasn't it?" To Ariel's utmost horror, Mike made his grand entrance. There was an underlying triumphant spark under his expression that Ariel detested. Joe seemed to have an 'oh-no' expression on his face while every one else looked with dread. "I knew there was something fishy. Ariel, you did so much just for me?"

He was clearly mocking her to his enjoyment. Ariel found herself stumped and speechless. She was not prepared for this yet.

"I'm really honored. I really am," Mike said, smiling. Ariel detected a kind of gladness in him, but she wasn't sure. Wait, why would he? If anything, he'd be glad that he still had that sort of influence on her. Something Ariel didn't appreciate.

"Don't flatter yourself, lover boy," Rainie hissed in defense of her friend. "She going out with Joe has absolutely nothing to do with you."

"I hope not. Because it would break my heart to know that I'd been hurting my girlfriend all this time," Mike said with suaveness, but from his tone, everyone knew he didn't mean it.

"Ex-girlfriend," Ariel corrected with a tinge of irritation.

"That is if only you really want that," Mike said, nearing towards her. He trapped her against a wall, eying her with intensity. Everyone was baffled at his words. What did he mean by that?

Ariel too was befuddled like a lost puppy. "What are you saying? You still have Hebe, remember? Don't you even think about cheating on her--"

"Look Ariel. There's this thing I've wanted to tell you about..." Mike said. All of the sudden, his taunting tone and mock vanished and what replaced them was a simple sincerity, something that astonished Ariel. What was he talking about? Ariel was too flabbergasted by all the possible ideas running through her head as he inched closer. "I'm really sorry for what I did, I really am. I'm sorry for being a jerk, but I had no choice--"

Ariel pushed him away, aware of his intention. Did he want to mend things with her when Hebe was not present? If there was anything that he proved to her, he proved that he was indeed a cheating bastard. "I don't want to hear it."

"No, Ariel. Listen. I really didn't want to go out with--"

"Shut up!"

What was this all about? What was he trying to do? Wasn't he enjoying himself at deriding and making fun of her just a moment ago? Why the sudden change? She remembered the other day when she met him; the way he had looked at her with infuriation when he thought she was going out with Joe. What was that all about?

She knew his personality pretty well. He was pretty contradictory, the type who likes to bully the girls he liked. It happened that way when he was courting her, always shooting biting teases. Mike wasn't very bright when it came to sentimental things, especially towards love. He didn't know that she liked him until the minute before they started going out.

Suddenly, a possibility dawned upon her.

Could Mike still harbor some feelings for her?

"Ariel, I swear, I really didn't want to break up with you. It's just that..."

Ariel gazed at Mike, who seemed so eager to explain his story. How many times had she wished for this scene to happen in the past few months? How many times had she pined for him in his absence? How many tears had she cried, and how many pains had she endured seeing him with his new girlfriend?

But now Ariel realized that this wasn't what she wanted anymore.

"Stop," Ariel said, interrupting him. "I don't want to hear what you have to say. It's over, Mike. It has been over since three months ago."

Mike paused short. They were so close that she could hear his shallow breathing. "You still have feelings for me, don't you?"

Ariel looked away. She knew she couldn't lie. "Yes."

A light gleamed in his face.

"But it's too late, Mike." Ariel turned to face him. "You have Hebe now."

"Listen, I didn't know why I was so stupid to break us up for her. But why didn't you say anything? Why didn't you stop me? I thought it was what you wanted since you already found out about it."

Ariel glared hard at him. "You were the one who said you didn't love me anymore! You cheated on me! What could I do?" She hated the way he blamed everything on her. "You made us this way!"

"But you could have said something!" Mike hissed, but knowing that his voice was slightly too loud, he immediately showed regret. "I... I was drunk that night. Hebe and I... I just couldn't abandon her after that, you know? She's, after all, a friend of mine. I couldn't hurt her so I--"

"So you decided to hurt me!" Ariel finished for him, pushing him away from her. Everybody was looking with much apprehension but Ariel didn't care about the attention she was getting. She couldn't accept the fact that her boyfriend put her feelings below everything else. He considered her even less than a friend! The revelation was too painful to absorb. "Listen, Mr. He! We have absolutely no relation from today onwards!"

"Ariel!"

"Don't call my name!" Ariel bellowed feverishly. "You're really despicable you know that? Hebe isn't here so you decided to tell me all of these things? For what? To get back with me? You stupid cheating bastard!"

"Go Ariel!" Rainie cheered. Then, she turned towards Mike with a deriding look. "You don't deserve her, bastard! Leave her alone, you already got a girlfriend!"

Mike ignored the biting remark. "I'm sorry I didn't make it clear to you earlier. I was out of my mind. I really wanted to get back with you, but I thought you were over me--"

"I AM over you," Ariel clarified.

Mike was stumped.

Joe smirked from the side. "Time to retreat in defeat, Two-timer," he icily suggested.

Pride wounded and publicly embarrassed, Mike turned an angry red. He turned towards Joe with a murderous look. "Stay out of my business! What was your motive in trying to be Ariel's fake boyfriend, huh?"

Joe was seemingly vexed but he didn't bother to reply. His snobby indifference only angered Mike more. With the intention to blast the arrogant jerk to hell, Mike sauntered over towards Joe and grabbed his collar. "You think you're so great? You're nothing but a lonely pile of feces to me with a rotten personality. Girls who swoon over you obviously are visibly challenged."

Ariel immediately stomped towards the two and pushed Mike away, getting in between them. Mike's insult was far too rude and harsh for Ariel to accept. She had the urge to defend Joe. "Wei! Watch your mouth. I know you're jealous, but you do not have to spit out such rubbish to humiliate yourself. Joe is a great person, smarter than you with a personality 100 times better than yours! If anyone's a pile of feces, it's you, you cheating crap!"

Joe glanced at her, a bit taken aback by her strong defense and endless praises. Since when was she so fond of him? He was puzzled, but nonetheless he didn't like the way she spoke as though she knew him or the way she tried to help him. Did he appear like a weakling to her?

Mike was baffled at Ariel's speech. He wasn't aware that she was so close to that Joe Cheng. "You've got to be kidding me comparing me to him."

"Actually you're right. You're such a low life that you don't even compare to him!" Ariel crossed her arms. Now she wondered why she ever pined for a bastard like him. How did she fall in love with him in the first place?! She must have been blind indeed.

"You'll regret this, Ariel Lin. I gave you a chance," Mike snarled.

"I am glad to not take that stupid chance! Going out with you was my biggest regret!”

"Oh, don't humor me, Ariel. Your biggest regret will be him, just you watch." Mike chucked them denigrating glares before taking off.

The tension immediately eased as if the air had been cleaned. Ariel sucked in a breath. "Oh my god. What drama," she muttered.

Rainie clapped aloud. "Bravo! Bravo! That was amazing!" She chirped, loving the entertainment show just broadcasted. "I knew I could count on you to brighten up my days."

Ariel frowned as she approached her friend. "You're shrilling now?! If it wasn't because of you, none of this would ever happen!"

"Hey... I made you see Mike's real personality, didn't I? Otherwise, you'd still pine for him." Rainie giggled. "And hey, I also match made you with a prince, didn't I? Instead of yelling at me, I think you should prepare me a big thank-you party."

Joe's classical annoyed expression surfaced again as he tossed his hands into his pockets. "Don't say rubbish. Didn't I clarify the matter already?"

"Joe!!!!" Suddenly, someone's deafening scream shattered through the perfect peace. At the entrance, Jolin Tsai was storming down the pathway with her clicking heels. She appeared angry with her dragon appearance.

Joe seemed to have a dreadful look on his face, probably knowing that another drama was coming.

"I heard that you're going out with this airhead, is it true?" Jolin asked.

Ariel watched Joe. Somehow, inside, she was wishing he'd say yes. It was a stupid wish and she even cursed herself for having such thought. Joe would never admit to that.

"Answer me, Joe! Please tell me no!" Jolin cried, tugging on his arm. "You can't do this to me."

Ariel almost felt pitiful for Jolin. Right now, she finally knew how pathetic it was to pine for someone who didn't really love you. From what just happened, Ariel could already conclude that her relationship with Mike had never been based on true love. Mike was just possessive by nature, instantly wanting to mend back with her once he learned she still had feelings for him. In a way, she could connect with Jolin and wished that there was something she could do.

"Jolin, let me go," Joe said irritably as he pulled his arm away.

"Please tell me, Joe. You have never gone out with anyone before; you can't do this!" Jolin was almost close to tears. Perhaps, she had always admired Joe for forever staying single and now she just couldn't accept anything to ruin her image of Joe.

"Who I go out with isn't your concern," Joe said coldly. "Look Jolin; I only think of you as a sister. Please don't make it harder than it is."

Jolin clasped a hand to her mouth as the tears finally spilled. Joe had never told her to go away like this. True that he had always demonstrated his irritation with her, but he made clear that their relationship was different than from the other girls' by not rejecting her willingness towards him. But now, he was clearly setting the lines between them as a brotherly and sisterly relationship--something that Jolin found immensely hard to accept at this point. "Joe, you can't do this," she sobbed. "I've been with you for a year now. You just can't break it off with me like this!"

"We've never gone out," Joe said, still maintaining his composure. "I never rejected you the way I did with the other girls because I considered you as a friend. Time has made me treasure you like a sister. But now, if you persist like this, then I'm afraid we can't even be casual acquaintances."

Jolin gazed at him with wet eyes, not believing his words."Is it... Is it because of her?"

Joe gazed at the puzzled Ariel for a second. For a very unknown reason, Joe approached Ariel and threw his arms on her shoulders. "Yes. As you said, we are going out. I hope that you don't have any wishful thinking towards me anymore."

Jolin was utterly defeated. She could not do anything but let the tears continue to flow down her cheeks like endless paths as she hurriedly dashed out of the room with humiliation and pain.

Ariel looked up to Joe, completely astonished by what just happened. Even Rainie and Gillian could not believe their eyes.

Joe released Ariel, looking tired and exhausted. Without saying another word, he walked away.

Ariel didn't know why, but she was compelled to chase after him.

"Wei, where you are going?"

"Class."

"But we have another five minutes or so."

Joe rolled his eyes. "Is there a rule that says you can't come to class early?"

Ariel kept silent as they walked down the path. He was clearly flabbergasted. Could it be because of Jolin? "Umm... I was wondering... Well... Back there...." Ariel stammered, not knowing how to phrase her thoughts. She was curious as to why he made things so heartbreaking for Jolin.

"I just feel like she needs a closure," Joe replied much to Ariel's surprise. How could he know what she was thinking?

"Huh?"

Joe glanced down at her, but he continued walking. "She's been by my side ever since I arrived here. She's always had this hope towards me that we'd be an item one day. It's sort of my fault actually. With me endlessly rejecting girls that hope grew day by day. I don't have any feelings for her but I didn't have the courage to break her heart the way I did with the other girls, for some very odd reason. I owed her this closure."

Ariel observed him. She didn't know that he was capable of thinking for other people with his I-don't-give-a-damn attitude, and so she was more than pleasantly surprised. "But you didn't have to use me..."

"Isn't that what you wanted? For that stupid challenge?”

Ariel bit her lips. "That is the least of my concern right now." He didn’t answer her, so she propped up a different topic. “Um, about Mike… Sorry about that.”

He glanced at her strangely. “Why?”

“I know you didn’t deserve that bashing… It’s all my fault. I’m sorry.”

“Stupid… you weren’t the one who said those things. But thanks for defending me back there.”

Ariel looked at him, sort of surprised that he said such polite words. “Oh… you’re welcome. He was slandering you. Of course I had to do that.”

Joe appeared annoyed again. “But don’t do that again. I’m not a weakling. I can speak up for myself.”

“Oh…” Ariel said disappointedly, biting her lips. Silence fell again.

"You really think Jolin will accept that?" Ariel asked another question.

He was quiet for awhile. "I don't know. Only time will tell."

"You know, this is the first time I see you so gentle with someone... Does Jolin have a place in your heart somewhere?" Ariel immediately regretted blurting out such a random question.

Joe smirked at her as he stopped in his tracks, making Ariel bewildered. He turned to face her. "Are you jealous?" he asked.

"What?!" Ariel widened her eyes. "Puh lease! I don't even like you!"

"Oh yeah? What's with the endless praises earlier? Someone clearly said I was a great person with a great personality." He taunted her.

Ariel sputtered. "I’m your friend. Why can’t I say those things?” She said defensively.

Joe laughed. If she wasn't correct, that was probably the first time she had ever heard him laughed. Ariel was completely astounded. "Why are you so anxious about it? Don't worry. Even if you like me, I would never ever fall in love with you."

Ariel glared at him. "Wei! Don't make yourself so high! I would be the one who would never ever fall in love with you!"

"Don't say things you're not sure of," Joe teased.

"Arg! I take back what I said! You have a rotten personality!" Ariel hissed.

"You can't take things back."

"Yes I can! Come back here!"

"Catch me if you can!"

"That's not fair. You have long legs!"

"What? Are you jealous of what God gave me too?"

Ariel fumed when he made faces at her. "You rotten pig! Oh my god! I must have been out of my mind saying those things about you! Come back here!"

Ariel couldn’t contain a strange happiness inside of her. She had taken notice that he did not reject her when she claimed that she was his friend. Although the change was subtle, Ariel knew that it was a huge progress.

Like two imbecilic kids, the two teens were racing down the hallway, thrusting verbal fireballs and teases.
