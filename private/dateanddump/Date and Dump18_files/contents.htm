Chapter 18

Confessions of the Heart

Jolin's words sped through Ariel's mind like an unstoppable racing car. She refused to believe in the idea that Joe may like her, but the more she considered Jolin's words, the more they made sense to her. She was right. You don't just kiss anyone you have no feelings for; even if someone asked you straight out for it, you just don't.

Her heart was thumping faster and faster as Ariel thought of the possibility. Jolin's fierceness had spanked her mind to think rationally. Right now, she wanted to confront Joe and ask him directly what the hell he was thinking when he returned her kiss. His face was so blank then but his eyes were expressive. Perhaps she misinterpreted them, but now she was willing to find out.

She was racing down the hallways, occasionally bumping into a few lingering bodies on the way, but in no way did it hinder her will to reach her destination. The music room.

Ariel knew he was in there because from ten feet away, she could hear the clear piano playing in a familiar tune. Her heart ached, knowing that the tune wasn't played for her, but she pushed it aside and continued forward. No matter what, she just had to choke out an answer from him.

She burst through the door, and there he was, perched on the seat in front of the piano.

She was panting loudly but her wheezing sound was lost in the melodious song. It was so sweet; she was sure that if anyone played the tune specifically for her, she'd melt right then and there.

She inched forward, her ears opening.

Suddenly, Joe stopped.

Ariel was staring at his back, wondering if he knew her presence.

"Are you here to give me a lashing?" Joe's cold voice sauntered to her ears, sending chills down her spine.

"No," she answered slowly, stepping closer. "I'm here to talk like a perfectly sane and civilized person."

"Okay, you have one minute."

Ariel's heart lurched fretfully. In a way, she felt like an idiot. Judging from his tone, it didn't sound like he wanted to listen to anything she had to say. Even with his back facing her, Ariel could sense his deadpanned expression, one that an average girl like her could never fathom. She could never guess what he could be thinking; he was a complete puzzle to her. Then again, maybe it was because he was so skillful when it came to hiding his emotions. Hadn't she accused him again and again that he was a fake?

Yet, she fell in love with such a person---a person who refused to express his feelings openly to anybody, who concealed his emotions deep within, and who was always bluntly cold toward her. Maybe it was because she had always been drawn to his mysteriousness. She loved playing that guessing game with him, didn't she? With her stubbornness, she had never abandoned him, but instead continuously thrived forward to reach out to him.

Even when he brushed her hands away, she still persisted. Part of the reason was due to the bet of course. The damned thing that started it all. But halfway through, the bet wasn't the only thing that occupied her mind. He. He was the other object that constantly invaded her head, terminating any effort to return to normality.

"Well?" He probed.

She gulped as panic threatened to consume her entirely. Why was it suddenly so hard to speak now? It was as though her voice just disappeared out of nowhere and it had to take her a moment to find it back as she rasped out, "I... I have something to ask you."

"Go ahead."

Wasn't he feeling anything at all? It always bothered her how he was able to remain cool and calm. Indifference had always been something he excelled to portray. For people like her, everything was just so blatantly obvious---her fears, her thoughts... They were easy to discern as though they were written all over her forehead.

"When you kissed me, what were you thinking?"

The moment she spat out those words, Ariel instantly wished she could take them back, think more thoroughly, and then rephrase so that it wouldn't sound so weird and awkward. However, what was to be asked had already been asked. Now she was just squirming in unease for his answer.

"What do you want me to say?"

She glared at his back. "The truth, of course!"

He whipped on his heels, his eyes now boring intensely into her amber ones. He approached her, facing her up close. Ariel could feel her thighs, knees, and calves turned into mush at the close proximity. Breathing threatened to stop. "What do you expect me to say?"

Her mind was so flustered, Ariel didn't know what to think. "I-I don't know... I just think that you must be thinking something when it happened. You know... just anything. Maybe I was too forceful…”

He chuckled softly at her, and it managed to put her at ease for a second. "You don't have to degrade yourself that way."

She gazed up at him. "Well, answer the damn question!"

"What if I don't want to?" He said.

"You..." How could he? She had swallowed her pride to come running back to him and ask politely, but he in return refused to answer? If there wasn't anything more infuriating than his tendency to conceal everything to himself! "Fine! Be that way!" Ariel snapped finally, shoving aside the reason why she came here in the first place. "I don't know why I was foolish to ever fall for a jerk like you. You know what, I don't care anymore! You can do whatever you want!" she fumed.

"You're leaving?"

Ariel turned away. "Yes! It's not like you care anyway, right? You might be exploding in relief that I am leaving you the hell alone!"

"You seem mad."

"Of course I am, you fool!" Ariel hissed. "You always ignore me. You always say stuff that hurt me. I hate you!" With one last huff, Ariel dashed toward the double doors, ready to leave and escape the despair and upsetting reality. She just wanted to go and find where she could hide herself, bury her embarrassment, and never come out to sunlight until all of this cruelty proved to be a just a plain nightmare.

His voice put a halt to her steps. "Stay."

"Why should I?" Ariel sniffed but she wasn't going to show him her tears. Hadn't she revealed enough about herself? Wasn't she ashamed enough?

"Listen to this song," he simply said and sat pensively on the stool as his fingers graced across the keys.

Ariel didn't want to, at first. She didn't want to listen to something that wasn't meant for her. Her legs were about to move along when all of a sudden, she stopped. Her ears picked up the notes as she listened carefully. There was something different about the song that tugged at her heartstrings. It wasn't the same song that she always caught him playing.It was more upbeat and lively. Before she knew it, her feet were riveted to the ground, and moving was almost impossible.

Two minutes passed by, and he finally stopped. Silence conquered the room.

Ariel scoffed under her breath. "I think you're playing it to the wrong person. I'm sure Selina would have been exhilarated to hear that." She began to move.

"Why do you think that?"

"What, you couldn't possibly play that for me," Ariel stated flatly, rolling her eyes.

"Funny," his voice said, "You're the only one listening."

She turned around, her eyes piercing. "All right. What do you want? I came here to talk to you, but you weren't cooperating. I leave, but you're not letting me. Mind telling me your motive?"

Joe didn't answer her. Again, his silence was grating her to no end. She watched as he got up from his seat and stood by the grand piano, looking down at the keys as his fingers wiped gracefully against them. "I had always loved piano since I was a child. It was the only form of expressing myself." There was a small pause as memories came flooding back to his mind. "Selina... She taught me how to play. She was a cute child back then, always so bubbly. She still is now."

Ariel couldn't believe that he was saying all of this. Did she look like she want to hear about his love story?

"I've always admired her because she seems to have everything I don't," he said solemnly. "But she didn't regard me as someone lower than her. In fact, she treated me quite the opposite, so caring, so sensitive. She was my only best friend back in primary school." There was a look of happiness on his face. It looked like whenever he mentioned Selina, he was able to express his adoration for her. "But then we separated and it was devastation for me. I had no one to talk to anymore. I've become depressed."

"She didn't know how I felt toward her, of course. Selina, that is." Joe continued. "It had always been my wishful thinking. How could a sunshine girl like her fall for a dark and gloomy boy like me? But still, I wanted to be by her side. I wanted to see her happy, because her smile brightened my days. But fate was cruel. My parents sent me to a new school, thinking that the bad environment had contributed to my autistic behavior. They didn't understand that I didn't want to go. They forced me. I hated them, then."

Even though, Ariel somewhat knew about his unhappy childhood, she had never expected that it would be so heartbreaking to hear. "Ever since then, I withdrew more from the world. I always brushed people off, thinking what's the use? Fate will probably make us friends, but it'll eventually tear us apart." He looked down on the piano again. "You see, even though I was in a new place, I never forget her. She was the only source to my sanity. Every day, I'd play piano. Playing it makes me feel at ease. It feels as though she's beside me."

Ariel felt her heart sank deeper and deeper into a pit of hopelessness. She was foolish to ever think that Joe could have any feelings toward her. Selina in his heart was irreplaceable.

"But you know what I found out?

Her ears perked up. "What?" she softly asked.

"All this while, I had been living in the past. I kept thinking about those days when I was happy to the extent that I forget about the present." He shook his head, as though pitying himself. "My admiration for Selina is undeniable, but what I have for her was supposed to be in the past. I didn't realize it yet until my birthday."

Ariel blinked. "Your birthday?"

"Well it should be, but I didn’t discover it until later."

"Oh? And what is that?" Ariel gulped in anticipation.

"I already love someone else."

Could he mean her? No... that couldn't be. Ariel refused to even harbor another hope. She'd just end up in severe disappointment. "Oh.. Good for you," she said flatly.

"Well?" Joe continued, raising his eyebrows. "Aren't you going to ask me who it is?"

"Can't be me," Ariel grumbled. "You've rejected me, remember?"

"I don't recall doing such thing."

"Don't play dumb," she snapped. "I kissed you, but you looked horrified. If that's not a sign, then I don't know what is."

"Have I ever told you you're rash in your conclusions?"

All of a sudden, Ariel felt unusual. The way he was staring at her was making her feel as though there were more meanings behind his words. She may not be academically inclined, but she wasn't stupid. "Are you trying to tell me something?"

"Yeah."

Ariel paused, not knowing what to say. The possibility was flashing inside her mind but she was too weak and afraid to think of it.

Joe saw her struggle and said, "The song I just played... Selina has never heard it before. I actually composed the song, thinking about someone else. Someone who's alway expressive in the way she carries herself. She's a bit violent, too but her liveliness makes up for it. She's perky, pretty cute, but unfortunately, a little brainless sometimes."

Ariel's eyes jerked open. "Excuse me?"

Something flickered across Joe's face. "Even right now, she's still pretty clueless."

"What--" Ariel paused, things beginning to connect together. She had a feeling of what he was implying but fear disallowed her to think foolishly. Ariel blinked again, apprehension in her eyes. In a moment, a smirk ran across her face. "Okay, then. Ready to tell me what you think of my kiss?"

"It was sloppy," he said.

Ariel felt her blood pulsing. "Wei! You don't have to be that direct!" she yelled, feeling the colors shading her cheeks.

"I haven't finished yet," Joe said, surprising her. "You asked me what I was thinking right? Here's my answer.My mind was pretty much blank then."

God... so she went through all that troubles just to get him to tell her that his mind was blank!?

"But you know what?" Joe continued. "My heart was racing."

Ariel suddenly froze. "Huh?"

"Yeah. It was," he confessed. If she did not mistaken, did she just discern a tinge of shyness on his face? "I never had that feeling before. Not even Selina made me feel that way." Okay... so this was a little unexpectant. Ariel wasn't sure if she was ready to hear it. How contradictory. Awhile ago, she was wishing for him to give her an answer that would make her leaping in joy, but right now, she somewhat dreaded hearing his voice. Maybe she was nervous? Maybe she was scared that it was again a false hope?

"Ahem. Okay..." Ariel felt a little awkward.

He pinned his eyes on her, causing her to almost have a heart attack. "You get what I mean, Ariel?"

"No... No, I don't," she said honestly, unsure. "Do you mean that you... like me?" God. Saying those words felt so childish. It was like they were little kids, confessing their feelings to each other. But then again, when does love ever discriminate? Young or old, children or adults, love can definitely wedge its way in without one's knowing. Mysterious and wondrous, wasn't that how love was?

He smirked at her. "Finally, got it huh? You're slower than I thought."

Her cheeks fumed. "Don't start, again!" She hissed, crossing her arms. Then, suddenly, her mind began registering. "Wait... what?"

"God, you're really stupid."

Ariel brushed off his insults, her need to understand growing more intensely. "But how?"

"I don't know," Joe said. "I guess your idiocy does rub off on me, so I've become an idiot without even realizing it."

"Hah! The genius admits that he's an idiot!" Ariel laughed, happiness suddenly invading her entire body.

“You make me feel good.” It took her awhile to figure that he was still explaining why he liked her. “I don’t know. But I just feel really comfortable around you. With you, I can almost spill any secrets.”

Ariel took notice of the ‘almost’ and was tempted to find out what it meant. “And…”

“And what?” he smugly smiled at her.

“What thing haven’t you told me?”

“You’re not that stupid,” he suddenly switched the topic. “You’re actually quite smart if you put in the effort.”

Ariel blushed. It wasn’t an everyday thing that Joe Cheng complimented you. “Of course, I have always known that…” she couldn’t help but boast.

“Really? Then why do you always get upset when I call you stupid?” He teased her.

Redness crept up to her face, but Ariel managed to hit him in the arm. “Wei! Why are you switching the topic! You didn’t answer my question!” She bawled.

“What question? I’ve answered them all.”

Ariel was about to blow up. “I’m really going to lose it-”

“I already told you all my secrets.”

Ariel looked at him, finally realizing it. “Oh…”

He only smirked at her.

"Well... umm.... what am I supposed to do or say now?" Although this wasn't her first time taking on a relationship, Ariel still considered herself inexperienced. Now she resembled a lost puppy. "What happened when I was with Mike?" she pondered out loud, plastering on a thinking face. He was after all her only boyfriend in her entire seventeen years of life.

Joe was suddenly beside her. Ariel almost jumped up in startling fright. "You know, mentioning about your ex does grate on your boyfriend's nerves."

Ariel blushed at his direct implication. Suddenly her face perked up. "Hey... is that why you've always acted so grouchy around me? Because you know that I was only after you because of Mike?"

"No."

"Come on! You must be, huh?" Ariel smiled as she continued teasing. "I mean... You probably fell for me about the same time as I did for you. But we didn't realize that yet. And I was always bringing up about the bet... Hmmm... Very fishy...."

"Come on. It's late. Let's go home," said Joe.

She trailed after him persistently. "Tell me! Am I right?"

"No--"

"Liar! Now that I think about it, it makes a whole lot of sense why you're always acting cold toward me--"

"Shut up, or I'll kiss you," he said.

Ariel gasped at the sudden closeness of his face, her heart thumping in an abnormal pace. Even though they were "official" now, there was still a feeling of awkwardness between them. She needed time to mold into this relationship and his sudden fierceness in expressing the idea of kissing her was frightening her bit.

Suddenly, he laughed. In the months of knowing him, she had never heard him laugh so heartily before. It made her smile that she was able to have that influence on him, but she hid her content as she feigned a pout. "Are you laughing at me?"

"Yeah."

"Gee, thanks." She mumbled sarcastically. "I have a boyfriend who constantly teases and insults me. I feel like the luckiest girl in the world."

"Quit moping."

"Oh, but I can't help it," Ariel said. "If someone doesn't insensitively says things that hurt me, maybe I wouldn't be."

Joe was now staring at her with an intensity Ariel found unusual. "Have I always appeared that mean to you?"

"You want the truth?"

"Mm hmm."

"Yes," Ariel breathed out. His face looked sullen for a second, as though he was trying to recall the moments in which he exercised barbarism toward her. A faint smile curled up on Ariel's lips as she continued, "But you know what? I fell in love you with that way. If you hadn't been mean to me, our relationship wouldn't exist. We would just be a boring 'fake' couple."

Joe smirked at her. "Oh, was that a compliment?"

"Don't get cocky," she snapped.

They sauntered down the empty hallways in silence, appreciating the moment to themselves. Never would they think that things would end up this way, but indeed, life was full of surprises. A worthless and ridiculous deal made by two bored teenager girls actually had altered a turning point in two people's lives.

Suddenly, something hurled at Ariel. "Joe?"

"Hmm?"

"What about Selina?"

The name hung in the air between them, hailing a moment of tension. Ariel knew how important Selina was to him and to request that he completely forget her would be absurd. Despite their relationship, Ariel didn't think she had the right to control his life. Even though it may sadden her, but Ariel was willing to accept anything he was about to tell her.

"It's a big mess now, isn't it?" She asked, remembering when Selina leaned in to kiss him. Perhaps she was right with her persistence in thinking that Selina had always treated Joe more than a brother. After what she saw, Ariel was convinced Selina did have feelings for Joe. "What are we going to do?"

"We'll just have to carry on," he simply said.

"Do you still like her?" Ariel asked softly, scared to hear the truth.

"Yes," he said.

Ariel instantly withdrew her arms, feeling the tears forming in the corner of her eyes. "Oh... Well.. Maybe you should tell her to be your girlfriend then." She couldn't help but sound like a jealous possessive partner, but Ariel had limits when it came to relationships. As far as she was concerned, she wasn't about to be willing to share her man.

Joe grabbed her shoulders, staring into her eyes. She felt naked under his intense gaze because she knew he could read everything. "Selina is a very important person in my life." Ariel had to look away now. She couldn't bear to see how his enamored eyes would spark. "But..."

Ariel waited for an answer, her heart beating rapidly.

"My feelings toward her had always been admiration and appreciation," he said. "It had never been anything more than that."

Now her eyes were wide open. "Really? But on your birthday, you clearly said--"

"I misunderstood myself," he cut her.

"Oh..." Ariel didn't know what to say. "So now what? Selina clearly does reciprocate your feelings."

"I don't want to hurt her," Joe said, "But it'll just have to be."

Ariel nodded in agreement, not knowing what else to say. After a few short silence, she spat out quickly, "Are you going to tell her?"

"Yeah."

Now the idea sounded absurd to her. "But.. wouldn't that be too cruel? She is a girl after all. It would be hard--"

"Do you rather have me hurt you?" Joe asked.

Ariel looked away. "I don't know."

"Don't worry about. Selina will understand," Joe said soothingly in an attempt to assure her.

As they proceeded, Ariel couldn't help but imagine all kind of scenarios in her mind. No matter how much Joe assured her that he felt nothing romantic with Selina, Ariel couldn't help doubting him.

Stop thinking, she scolded herself. You must trust Joe.

Yes she must.
