Chapter 2

How to Seize Your Chance (and humiliate yourself)

“For the last time, NO!”

Ariel broke free from Rainie’s suffocating grasp. She tried to pay attention to the boring musical show but her friend would not let her. Well, that was expected. When Rainie got bored, she’d go crazily hyper and jumpy at the expense of her audience’s sanity.

“Come on, Ariel,” Rainie drawled. “You have to take on my challenge. I think it’s the best one I’ve come up for the past seventeen years of my life!”

“Oh, so you’ve been making up challenges since you were born?” Ariel asked sarcastically.

“I was born to rock your world, Ariel dear,” Rainie said a bit too sweetly. Ariel felt her skin crawl with goose bumps.

“I don’t want to.”

“Don’t be so stubborn.”

“No means no!”

“SHH! Lower your voice.” Gillian hushed the two, losing her patience. “I’m trying to watch the show.” But no matter how absorbed she was by the classical music, she still spared some of her consciousness towards what the two were discussing about. “But keep going.”

Ariel and Rainie rolled their eyes.

“Come on, why not?” Rainie whined, not willing to submit.

“Why should I?!” Ariel retorted.

“Because… Because my life is boring,” Rainie replied. “Help me make it more entertaining?”

How far could one go for entertainment? It was a question impossible for Ariel to answer before, but now it couldn't be more obvious. “But I’m not ready to date--”

“Besides,” Rainie continued without letting the girl finish. “You never turn down any of my challenges. Why are you so stubborn this time? Unless…” Her eyes narrowed as suspicion blared all across her face. “Unless… you’re still not over Mike.” She finished with an air of mystification.

Ariel’s eyes widened like saucers. “No!” She practically screamed, catching on the attention of the teachers standing nearby. “I mean, please. I am over him three months ago.”

“That’s what you say,” Rainie said, “But in your heart…” She trailed her finger and pointed it at Ariel’s heart, just for the sake of suspense. “You’re pining for him. Listen, I can hear the desirous beating of your heart. Thump, thump, thump—“

Ariel slapped her finger away. “Forget it, Rainie. I’m not going to-”

“Why not, Ariel?” Gillian spoke, her eyes still glued to the kid playing the clarinet with skills on stage. “You haven’t had a boyfriend for three months. Don’t you miss having a relationship?”

“I don’t see YOU going out with anybody-”

“You’re definitely not over your ex,” Gillian concluded. “When a person is unwilling to get into a new relationship, it means they’re still living in the shadows of the previous one-”

“I am not!” Ariel cried. “What do you know about relationships, anyway, Gillian?”

“Prove it,” Rainie said with a grin. “Are you really over your ex? Date the school’s cold prince and prove it.”

Ariel looked infuriatingly at her two friends, who were mulishly resolute on pushing her against the wall. She was sick of having people assume that she was still in love with Mike. For God’s sake, he had a new girlfriend already! He was probably having the hell of his time making out with her at this moment. My God. That was right. While he was having fun, what was she doing? Rotting in the singledom.

“Fine. You want proof, you got it. I’ll take your stupid challenge,” Ariel decided, instantly injecting excitement in her friends’ nerves. She was determined to show everyone including Mike that she was completely and absolutely OVER him.

“Yes! Now this is the Ariel I know!” Rainie squealed.

“SHHH!” The teachers shot the girls daggers.

“But why the cold prince?” Ariel asked, whispering. She remembered her top three reasons why she wouldn’t date him.

“Because he is the most challenging, duh!”

“But--“

“Okay, so here are the rules.” Rainie didn't even let the poor Ariel finish her sentence. “You have to ask him out. We all know that the cold prince will reject anybody that asks him out; so therefore the real challenge is to come up with a way to make him NOT reject you.”

Ariel let out a big sigh, but it went unknown to the excited talking Rainie, oblivious to everything but her own voice. There was just simply no way to turn this down judging from Rainie's persistent character. “You have three months to do it. We'll end it right on Valentine's Day. If you win this, then you totally proved to us that you are over Mike. I'll kneel down to you three times.” Rainie's eyes brightened up. But the spark in them told Ariel that Rainie was confident that she was going to LOSE this time.

“If you lose however...” Rainie trailed off in suspense, eyeing Ariel with sinister thoughts. “You're going to have to strip in front of the entire school.”

"What?!" Ariel and Gillian both shouted, eyes inflating to the size of balloons.

Rainie laughed humorously. "Oh, look at you two! I was kidding!"

Ariel shot daggers at her friend. "How dare you joke like that! My reputation is not to be tainted with! Hmphh."

"C'mon, can't you take a joke," Rainie poked her friend. "Okay, if you lose, you're going to have to pose in a bikini for our class to see."

"What is the difference from the one before?!" Ariel shouted, outraged. "Rainie! Sometimes, I doubt that you're my friend!"

"Hey!!" Rainie rebuked. "You say that again! The reason I'm so persistent with this challenge is to help you get over Mike! I'm not a friend?" She let of a heavy angry scoff.

"If you were my friend, you'd know that I was over Mike long ago!"

"No! It's because I'm your friend that I know you're NOT over Mike!"

The girls looked at each other with a hot glare before tearing their eyes from each other. To demonstrate their discontent and adversary, they crossed their arms. This scene was so familiar that Gillian had to suppress a sigh. Ever since they were friends, Rainie and Ariel had a habit of arguing every now and then, relentless and stubborn as mules. It would take more than a cow to make one of them submit defeat to the other. And even if one of them did, the compromise was bloody.

Gillian tugged on both of her friends, wanting to cease the growing fire. "Hush! The teachers are looking at us. Stop your childish feud, please?"

"Tell her to go to hell, please?!" Ariel asked Gillian.

Rainie's eyes widened. "Gillian. Tell HER to go to hell please?!"

Gillian sighed. She could feel a headache surfacing at her friends' pettiness. "You two resemble a married couple!" She commented, knowing the remark will toss both girls in disgust.

"No way!" Both girls cried.

"See, that's why you two shouldn't fight," Gillian reasoned. "Now, let's talk like the civilized people we are. Get back to the challenge."

Rainie and Ariel exchanged glances, but they both obliged Gillian's order, since they knew the fight will get them nowhere.

"As I was saying..." Rainie trailed off, seeing Ariel's sharp gaze on her. "Okay fine... I'll change the conditions. If you lose.... hmmm." She scrunched in deep thought, trying to rack her brain for an appropriate but interesting punishment. She had always been a person who'd go for the craziest and funniest experiments and no way was she going to let this challenge launch off with boring conditions. "You have to kiss my brother."

"RAINIE!!" Both girls screeched.

"Okay, fine, fine! The loser will have to sing children nursery rhymes for the whole school to hear!!" Rainie sighed with disappointment.

Ariel grinned with satisfaction.

"That's better," Gillian approved.

"Deal," Ariel said.

"Man, you guys are so boring!" screamed Rainie.

Before the girl could complain any further, the MC announced that Joe Cheng was going to play a piano piece. Almost instantly, Ariel saw everyone snapping their eyes open as though his name was a magic wand that cast the awakening spell. Ariel, too, was intrigued.

Joe bowed to the audience with an indecipherable expression. Ariel studied him. Okay, so he wasn’t bad looking, but he wasn’t that spectacular either. She wondered why the whole school was so smitten with this guy. What, did he have some cute foxy ears that everybody else didn’t?

He sat at the piano, seemingly absorbed in his own world as his fingers ran across the keyboard, producing enchanting music that enamored the crowds. Ariel looked at him, stunned. He was the so-called Cold Prince of the school, yet right now, she discerned neither coldness nor arrogance from him. In fact, he seemed as though he was a person of warmth and emotions--an image that shocked Ariel. Ariel was mesmerized as she allowed his beautiful music to travel through her ears.

His piece was so poignant she could feel her heart prick along with the tears it was crying. She watched Joe as he slid his fingers against the piano with a passion she never knew existed. Somehow, it brought memories of Mike back into her mind, overwhelming her with abrasive emotions.

How could a person who always appeared frosty play such an evoking piece?

It was as if he had suffered a pain similar to hers…

When he finished the piece, the whole auditorium bounced off with loud applause. Ariel found herself clapping along.

“Oh my God! Not only is he a champion at basketball, he’s a star at playing the piano! Where do you find such a guy!?” Rainie squealed. “Now, aren’t you glad you took the challenge? That Joe Cheng doesn’t seem that ruthless as people claimed.”

“I guess not…” Ariel found herself already warming up to him.

“Ariel, don’t you reckon you should go backstage now?” Rainie asked.

“Huh? Why?”

“I’d better get you started before you back down on the challenge. You have to seize your chance! There’s probably a mob of girls back there waiting for him. Go!”

“But…” Ariel stuttered, not at all prepared. “This is too abrupt! He’s going to reject me straight out!”

“Not if you use tricks!” Rainie giggled. “Here, pull your skirt up a bit… and unbutton the first few buttons of your blouse… Yeah that’s right. You looking sexy, mama.”

Ariel slapped Rainie’s roaming hands away. “Mind you, I am NOT prostituting myself for a bet!”

“Hmm, I don’t think Ariel stands a chance,” Gillian scrutinized, pushing up her glasses.

“And why not, Miss Know-It-All,” Rainie asked.

“If you come up to him like this, he’d probably just think you’re a slut. Before you know it, he’d push you out of his sight.”

Ariel and Rainie exchanged glances. “Then, what do you suggest her to do?”

“He rejects anybody who asks him out. God knows how many hearts he’d broken. By now, he has probably already encountered all the possible courting scenes. If you want to win, you’d have to do something different. Something he has never encountered before…”

“Get to the point!” Rainie snapped.

“At this rate, there’s only one thing we can do,” Gillian said with Rainie tapping her fingers on the armrest. “Use the guilt card.”

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ariel could not believe she was doing this. This was outrageous. She had just only regretted her stupid decision when all the adrenaline faded.

Rainie, that girl! How in the world did Ariel end up with having her as a best friend? There was a growing desire to turn back but when she imagined how Rainie would react, Ariel immediately shuddered away the thought. She had already come this far. Only a coward would quit now. And she wasn’t that.

The mob of girls were screaming Joe’s name, waving banners. Ariel couldn’t believe the chaotic sight. Was Joe an upcoming star or what?! With that kind of fandom, Ariel couldn’t resist thinking so.

Okay, gotta drive those girls away… Ariel thought. She snapped her fingers when an idea hit her.

“GIRLS, GIRLS!” Ariel screamed, making her way through the crowd. “I heard that Joe just snuck out through the south exit! He was with a girl! Can you believe that?! We must get them back. Come on, let’s go!” Ariel waved her hands towards the south and all the girls sauntered towards the exit in aggravation and bashing of the anonymous (imaginary for Ariel) girl.

Ariel snickered as she ventured into the interior of the backstage. It took her quite a few minutes, but once she caught sight of him talking to a teacher, Ariel’s heart thrashed.

‘Okay, calm down Ariel. I’ll just have to pretend bumping into him. And then, I’ll cry him a river. He’d feel guilty, and before I know it, he’d fall in love with me.’ Ariel couldn’t help but giggle.

Ariel readied herself in her position. She took a peek, seeing him heading straight at her direction. She was nervous as heck. Sucking in a breath, she counted one, two, three….

And dashed out of her position.

CRASH!!!!!

Ariel smacked right into a cabinet at a speed of a racing car. It shook and rattled as shock traveled through every part of its interior. But the real sufferer wasn’t the cabinet but the wordless Ariel who was stunned (and pained) out of her wits.

Oh my god… wrong target! She thought inside her head.

Joe had staggered back several steps when she bolted out. Surprise was evident on his face, but it quickly disappeared when it emerged. He only stood there and observed her.

‘Get up, quick!’ She ordered herself. Ariel quickly snapped out of her trance and let out a bellowing cry, improvising crazily in her head.

“Oh my god! My hands! My hands!” Ariel squealed pretending that her hands were broken. “Painful, painful!! My god, they’re broken! Broken…” she wept.

Joe only looked at her with a bored glance.

“My beautiful hands!” She wailed exaggeratedly. “How will I ever play the piano again? My god, my dream’s gone… Gone… Just like that…”

From the corner of her eyes, she glimpsed him rolling his eyes. ‘Crap, was my acting that bad?’ Ariel thought with a worried expression.

Joe didn’t pay any more attention to her. Tossing his hands into his pockets, he strode right pass her.

Ariel froze for a millisecond before she registered what just happened. Did he just blatantly ignore her after all of her crying? Ariel could not contain her disbelief. He could have at least asked her if she was okay! For God’s sake, she did crash into a hard object!

But false to what she hoped, Joe did not turn back. Angry at his snobby behavior and negligence, Ariel found herself storming towards behind him with a finger pointed at his back. “Wei! You stop right there!” She yelled.

Surprisingly he did.

Ariel took the chance to stomp in front of him. “Did you notice how hurt I was?! Why didn’t you say anything?”

Joe chucked her an odd glance, but she couldn’t decipher much of it. “That’s your business,” he coldly said.

Ariel was stunned for a moment. “What do you mean it’s my business? For your information, it was YOUR fault!” She snapped.

“Funny, I didn’t make you crash into that cabinet.”

Ariel pointed a finger at him, revulsion thundering down her nerves. “You…”

“I thought you said your hands were broken?” Joe asked in a taunting tone.

She quickly hid her hands behind her, spluttering for something to say. “S-say sorry now!”

“Don’t you have anything better to do? You put in so much to act all of that shit out to do what? Get my attention? For your information, I don’t say sorry to people like you.” With a cold glare that gnawed all electrical activity of her heart, he sauntered away.

Ariel whipped around, staring at his retreating back with multiplied resentment thundering down her nerves. Even the way he walked spoke of majestic arrogance! She couldn’t believe how much she humiliated herself for him, how she brainlessly said crap.

Suddenly, he stopped in his tracks. “Oh yeah. If you were planning to ask me out, well, sorry to break it to you, but I’m going to have to reject you.”

Ariel’s eyes widened, bewildered at his assumption. But what angered her the most was his tone that rang with deafening insincerity.

He didn't even sound apologetic. If anything, he sounded like he didn't give a damn. Although Ariel harbored no real feelings for this man, his harsh rejection hurt nonetheless. It suddenly occurred to her that she was like the other girls at their school. It was just her turn right now to suffer what they had.

“Wei! You got it wrong mister! I didn’t ask you out, so technically, you didn’t reject me!” Ariel yelled with all of her might, anger pouring out through her words.

She breathed rapidly as she witnessed his wordless leave. He was so confident of himself that he assumed she was chasing after him. How infuriating! Ariel had never felt so insulted in her span of seventeen years of life. As she sucked in a breath, the reality of the situation finally slammed down upon her, making her shake with hard realization.

For the next three months, she was going to have to pursue him, the alleged Cold Prince.

Oh God, what had she done to herself?
